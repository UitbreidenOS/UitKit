# Freelancer Weekly Workflow

A repeatable Monday-to-Friday process for independent consultants and freelancers — using Claude Code to handle client communications, project delivery, business development, and admin in a structured weekly cadence.

---

## Overview

This workflow structures your week into five focus areas: client delivery, client communications, business development, financial admin, and planning. It takes approximately 2-3 hours of overhead time per week — the rest is your billable work.

**The principle:** Admin expands to fill available time. This workflow time-boxes it.

---

## Monday — Planning and Client Kick-off (45-60 minutes)

### Step 1: Weekly planning (15 minutes)

Before opening email, answer three questions:
1. What must be delivered this week? (client obligations)
2. What business development action will I take this week? (one outreach or follow-up)
3. What is my financial position? (do I have any invoices past due?)

```
/cash-flow-forecast

Quick Monday morning check:
- Invoices due this week: [list any]
- Expected income this week: $[X]
- Upcoming expenses: [any]
- Current cash balance: $[X]

Any urgent cash flow issues I should address today before getting into client work?
```

### Step 2: Client status reports (20 minutes)

For every active project, send a weekly status update Monday morning. Clients appreciate proactive communication — it eliminates most check-in calls.

```
/client-status-report

Weekly status — [Client Name] — [Project Name]
Week of: [this week's date range]

Completed last week:
[List from your time tracker or memory]

Working on this week:
[Your plan]

Blocked on / need from client:
[Anything you need to make progress]

Status: [Green / Yellow / Red]
```

Send each report. Log sent time. If a client calls about the project this week, you have a paper trail.

### Step 3: Monday client check-ins (optional, 15 minutes)

For ongoing retainer clients, a brief Monday message maintains the relationship without requiring a formal status report:

```
Short Monday touchpoint for retainer client [name].

Context: [what you're working on for them this week]
Tone: Warm, brief, colleague-to-colleague

Write a 2-3 sentence message that says: you're on it this week, here's what they can expect, any specific request or check-in needed?
```

## Tuesday-Thursday — Delivery Days

Delivery days are primarily billable work. Claude Code supports them when you need it:

### On demand: complex communication

When you need to communicate something sensitive to a client:

```
/client-status-report

Yellow flag situation to communicate:
[Describe the issue]
[What caused it]
[What your plan is]
[What you need from the client]

Write a professional message that takes ownership, shows a plan, and maintains confidence. Not defensive. Not over-apologetic.
```

### On demand: new project discovery

When a prospect reaches out mid-week:

```
/freelancer-proposal

New inquiry received:
[Paste their message]

I need to:
1. Understand their actual need (ask 2-3 clarifying questions)
2. Give a rough scope/range without committing too early
3. Move toward a discovery call

Draft a reply that does this. Under 150 words. Confident but not pushy.
```

### On demand: scope creep response

When a client asks for something outside the agreed scope:

```
/scope-of-work

My client just asked for [describe the additional request]. 

This is not in our original SOW. Our change order rate is $[X]/hour.

Write a professional response that:
1. Acknowledges their request warmly
2. Notes that it falls outside the current scope
3. Proposes a change order at $[X] for approximately [X hours]
4. Asks whether they'd like to proceed

Firm but not adversarial. I want to do the work — I just need it to be properly scoped.
```

## Friday — Admin, Business Development, and Planning (60-90 minutes)

### Step 1: Invoice outgoing work (20 minutes)

Every Friday, invoice anything billable from the week.

For time-based billing:
- Pull your time tracking report (Toggl, Harvest, Clockify)
- Create invoices in your invoicing tool
- Note the invoice numbers for your records

For overdue invoices — Friday is follow-up day:

```
/invoice-chaser

Invoice #[X] — $[X] — [X] days overdue
Client: [name]
Payment terms: [Net 15/30]
Prior follow-ups: [none / [dates of prior contact]]

Draft the appropriate follow-up for [X] days overdue.
```

Escalation schedule:
- Day 1-7 overdue: polite reminder
- Day 8-21 overdue: firm professional follow-up
- Day 22-45 overdue: formal notice with late fee reference
- Day 45+: final notice or hand to collections

### Step 2: Business development (20 minutes)

One prospecting action per week, every week. Even when fully booked — the pipeline dries up in 90 days if you stop.

```
/cold-outreach

This week's prospecting target:
[Company or type of client]
[Contact name and title, if known]
[Why they might need my services]
[My relevant work or result to reference]

Write a personalized outreach email. 100 words max. Specific observation about their business, not a generic pitch.
```

Track responses in a simple spreadsheet: outreach date, contact, response (yes/no/later), outcome.

### Step 3: Weekly review (10 minutes)

Five questions to answer every Friday:

1. **What did I deliver this week?** (list, don't skip)
2. **What did I invoice?** (total this week)
3. **What's outstanding?** (unpaid invoices)
4. **What's in the pipeline?** (proposals out, conversations active)
5. **Am I on track for this month's revenue target?**

```
/cash-flow-forecast

End-of-week check:
- Invoiced this week: $[X]
- Cash received this week: $[X]
- Outstanding invoices: $[X] total ([breakdown by client])
- Pipeline (probability-weighted): $[X]
- Monthly target: $[X]
- MTD revenue: $[X]

Am I on track? What do I need to do next week to hit my target?
```

### Step 4: Next week planning (10 minutes)

Before closing Friday:
- Write your three priorities for next Monday
- Check for any deliverables due next week and confirm you're on track
- Reply to any proposal or contract items that need a response before Monday

## Monthly add-ons (first Friday of each month)

### Monthly financial review

```
/cash-flow-forecast

Monthly review — [Month]:
- Revenue this month: $[X] (target: $[X])
- Top 3 clients by revenue: [list]
- Largest expense categories: [list]
- Net income this month: $[X]
- Next 90 days cash forecast: [provide the inputs]

What should I focus on next month to improve my financial position?
```

### Portfolio and case study update

```
For the project I just completed for [client]:
- What did I deliver?
- What results did they get (if known)?
- What would make this a good portfolio case study?

Write a 150-word portfolio case study for my website. No client names if confidential. Focus on the problem, my approach, and the outcome.
```

### Rate review

Once a quarter, ask yourself:
- How full is my pipeline? (Full = raise rates)
- What is the market rate for my work? (Research 3 competitors or check communities)
- Am I taking work I wouldn't have taken a year ago? (Bad sign = you need to raise rates)

```
/ceo-advisor

I want to review my rates for the coming quarter.

Current situation:
- Services: [what you do]
- Current rate: $[X]/hour or $[X] per project type
- Pipeline: [full / comfortable / thin]
- Client quality: [good / mixed / some bad ones I take because I need the money]
- Market research: [any intel on what peers charge]

Should I raise rates? By how much? For which services? How should I communicate it?
```

## Feast/famine prevention

The biggest freelancer failure mode: too busy to do business development, then no work when projects end.

**The rule:** Do one outreach action every week, regardless of how busy you are. The pipeline has a 60-90 day lag. The work you win today starts in 2-3 months.

**Monday trigger:** If you are fully booked for the next 4 weeks, do business development anyway. If you are less than 80% booked for next month, do 3 outreach actions this week, not 1.

Use `/cold-outreach` to make this fast:
- Monday morning: identify 1-3 targets from your network or past inquiry list
- Draft outreach using the skill
- Send before starting billable work

Track in a simple table: Week | Target | Message sent | Response | Outcome.

## Templates to have ready

Build these once using Claude, then reuse and edit:

**Proposal template** — Your standard proposal structure for your most common project type. Fill in the client-specific details, use the template for structure.

**SOW template** — Your master scope of work. Client-specific details vary; structure and legal language are reusable.

**Status report template** — Your standard weekly update format. Consistent format builds trust over time — clients know what to expect.

**Invoice follow-up sequence** — 3 email templates: 7-day reminder, 21-day firm follow-up, 45-day final notice.
