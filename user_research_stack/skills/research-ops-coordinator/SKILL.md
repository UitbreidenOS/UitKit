## When to activate

- Setting up or improving research operations infrastructure
- Managing participant recruitment panels and databases
- Building a research insight repository
- Selecting and managing research tools and vendors
- Creating research templates and playbooks for non-researchers

## When NOT to use

- For conducting individual studies
- For analyzing specific study data
- For writing research reports

## Instructions

1. **Audit current state.** What tools, templates, and repositories exist? What's working, what's not?
2. **Build participant panel.** CRM-style database: demographics, participation history, no-show rate, preferences.
3. **Create research repository.** Central location for: plans, guides, recordings, insights, personas. Tag by project and theme.
4. **Standardize templates.** Research plan, interview guide, synthesis framework, insight report — reusable across studies.
5. **Manage tooling stack.** Evaluate and maintain: recruitment tools, recording/transcription, analysis tools, repository.
6. **Establish governance.** Consent management, data retention policies, privacy compliance (GDPR, CCPA).
7. **Democratize research.** Create lightweight playbooks for PMs and designers to run their own research with guardrails.

## Example

```
Research Ops Setup:

Tools Stack:
- Recruitment: User Interviews + internal panel (2,400 participants)
- Recording: Zoom + Otter.ai transcription
- Analysis: Dovetail (tagging + affinity mapping)
- Repository: Notion (linked to project pages)

Templates Library:
- Discovery research plan template
- Usability test plan template  
- Interview guide template (discovery, usability, evaluative)
- Insight report template
- Research readout deck template

Governance:
- Consent forms: digital + verbal, stored 2 years max
- Recording: encrypted, auto-delete after 90 days unless flagged
- Participant incentive: $75/hr, tracked in finance system
```
