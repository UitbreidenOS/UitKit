# Marketplace Monetization

Claudient's monetization model supports creators, users, and the platform ecosystem through transparent, flexible tier options and revenue sharing.

---

## Tier Structure

### Free Tier

**Cost:** $0  
**Best for:** Individual developers, learning, open-source contributors

**Features:**
- Install unlimited free stacks (MIT, Apache 2.0, CC-BY-SA-4.0, AGPL)
- Access to community forum and GitHub discussions
- Basic marketplace browsing and search
- Monthly email digest of new stacks

**Monetization (for creators):**
- No direct revenue
- Community reputation and brand building
- Potential client referrals and consulting opportunities
- GitHub sponsorship links supported

**Ad placement:**
- Non-intrusive: marketplace footer ads (partner tools, services)
- Optional in-stack recommendations (e.g., "Try X for faster setup")
- No user tracking or profiling

---

### Pro Tier

**Cost:** $9/month (or $85/year, save 21%)  
**Best for:** Serious practitioners, agency freelancers, small teams

**Features:**
- Everything in Free +
- Priority access to new stacks (48-hour early release)
- 3–5 premium stacks per month (advanced workflows, specialized domains)
- Ad-free marketplace experience
- Priority support (email, Discord)
- Stack version history and rollback
- Private marketplace (invite-only team access)

**Monetization (for creators):**
- Paid stacks monetized via **70/30 revenue share** (Year 1) or **80/20** (Year 2+)
- Pro Tier users have access to more premium offerings → higher revenue potential
- Featured placement for Pro-tier qualifying stacks

**Revenue model for creators:**
- Monthly subscription model: users pay $X/month for your stack
- Minimum pricing: $5/month or $50/year per stack
- Annual billing option increases subscriber lifetime value

**Example creator earnings:**
- Stack price: $15/month
- Pro subscribers: 25
- Monthly revenue: 25 × $15 = $375
- Creator earnings (70%): $262.50/month
- Annual run-rate: $3,150–$3,780

---

### Enterprise Tier

**Cost:** Custom pricing (typically $99–$499/month)  
**Best for:** Organizations, agencies, compliance-heavy domains

**Features:**
- Everything in Pro +
- Admin dashboard: team management, audit logs, SSO
- API access for programmatic stack distribution
- SLA: 99.9% availability, priority incident response
- Dedicated account manager
- Custom integrations and hooks
- White-label stack support (your branding)
- Volume licensing for teams (10–1000+ users)

**Monetization (for creators):**
- Custom revenue share negotiated per stack (typically 75–90%)
- Enterprise features enable higher-value stacks
- Co-marketing and case study opportunities
- Direct sales support (Claudient sales team assists)

**Revenue model for creators:**
- Enterprise licensing: flat fee, per-seat, or usage-based
- Minimum deal size: $500/month
- Annual contracts encouraged; Claudient handles billing

**Example creator earnings:**
- Enterprise stack: compliance monitoring + custom integrations
- License fee: $2,500/month per customer
- 2 Enterprise customers signed
- Monthly revenue: 2 × $2,500 = $5,000

## Revenue Share Models

### Subscription Model (Most Common)

User subscribes to your stack:
- **Monthly:** Recurring billing, cancel anytime
- **Annual:** Discounted rate, auto-renew
- **Free trial:** Optional 14-day trial (you set)

**Earnings calculation:**
```
Monthly MRR = subscriptions × price
Creator share = MRR × split % (70% or 80%)
```

**Example:**
- 50 active subscriptions at $15/month
- Monthly revenue: 50 × $15 = $750
- Year 1 creator earnings (70%): $525/month = $6,300/year

**Churn & retention:**
- Typical SaaS churn: 5–10% monthly
- Survival curve: 50% of users renew after 6 months
- Growth strategies: updates, community engagement, case studies

---

### Volume/Licensing Model (Enterprise)

Organizations license your stack for multiple users:

**Per-seat licensing:**
- $50–200 per user per year
- 10–100+ user minimum
- Annual contract with true-up (true usage at renewal)

**Example:**
- Organization with 50 engineers
- Per-seat price: $100/year
- Revenue: 50 × $100 = $5,000
- Creator earnings (80%): $4,000

---

### Freemium Model (Advanced)

Combine free + paid for conversion:

1. **Tier 1: Free (Starter Stack)**
   - Public, open-source, drives adoption
   - Targets large user base (build moat)

2. **Tier 2: Paid (Pro Stack)**
   - Advanced skills, premium integrations
   - $15–50/month
   - Targets engaged 2–5% of free users

3. **Tier 3: Enterprise (Custom Stack)**
   - Dedicated support, SLA, integrations
   - $2,500+/month per customer

**Revenue potential:**
- 1,000 free users → 50 pro users → 2 enterprise users
- Free: $0 revenue
- Pro (50 × $20): $1,000/month × 70% = $700
- Enterprise (2 × $3,000): $6,000/month × 80% = $4,800
- **Total: $5,500/month creator earnings**

## Certification & Revenue Impact

### Certified Badge Requirements

Your stack earns the **Certified** badge if:
- 3.5+ average user rating (5-star scale)
- 100+ installs within 60 days of launch
- 0 critical security or functionality bugs in last 90 days
- Updates within last 60 days
- Author responds to issues within 2 weeks

**Benefits of Certification:**
- 40–60% higher search visibility (algorithm boost)
- Featured in "Trending" and "Recommended" sections
- Co-marketing: featured in weekly newsletter (50K+ subscribers)
- Marketplace homepage rotation (1-month features)

**Revenue impact:**
- Certified stacks typically see 2–4x higher conversion
- Example: 10 subscriptions → 40 subscriptions after certification
- Payback period: 2–3 months of elevated visibility

### Losing Certification

Certification revoked if:
- Rating drops below 3.5 stars
- 3+ critical bugs reported unfixed for 30 days
- No updates for 90+ days
- Negative user feedback (spam, malware, misleading content)

**Recovery:**
- Address reported issues within 60 days
- Update stack with significant improvements
- Rebuild reputation: 30 days clean record to recertify

## Anti-Fraud & Dispute Resolution

### Prohibited Revenue Generation

You cannot:
- Generate fake subscriptions or install counts
- Use bots to inflate ratings or downloads
- Manipulate revenue share through refund schemes
- Bypass payment processing to avoid split
- Sell access to user data or analytics
- Promote competing stacks or services in your stack

**Violation consequences:** Immediate account suspension, earnings forfeiture, legal action.

---

### Refund Policy

**User refunds (30-day guarantee):**
- Users can request full refund within 30 days of purchase
- Processed via Stripe (you do not handle refunds directly)
- Refund reduces your earnings for that month

**Chargeback disputes:**
- User disputes charge with credit card issuer
- Stripe arbitrates (provides evidence)
- Disputed amount held in your account pending resolution
- Claudient does not absorb chargebacks; creator absorbs risk

**Appeal process:**
- If chargeback ruled in user's favor: refund issued
- If ruled in your favor: earnings released
- Excessive chargebacks trigger account review

## FAQ

**Q: Do I have to pay Claudient to list a free stack?**  
A: No. Free stacks are listed for free. You earn through reputation, referrals, and sponsorships.

**Q: Can I change my stack price after launch?**  
A: Yes. Price changes take effect at next renewal for existing users. Refund existing users if you lower price significantly.

**Q: What if I earn less than $20/month?**  
A: Earnings accrue until you hit $20. Then payout is triggered on the 15th of next month.

**Q: Can I sell my stack or transfer ownership?**  
A: You can transfer to another creator (new revenue share agreement required). You cannot resell on other platforms while in exclusive deal with Claudient (check your contract).

**Q: What happens if my stack gets delisted?**  
A: Existing subscribers continue until renewal; you earn revenue share during transition. You can relist after addressing issues.

**Q: How do I offer discounts or coupons?**  
A: Use Stripe's coupon codes (e.g., 20% off for 3 months). Revenue share applies to discounted price.

**Q: Can I offer a free trial?**  
A: Yes. Set 14-day free trial in Stripe. Trial users don't generate revenue until converting to paid.
