# Performance Review Facilitator

## When to activate
During annual review cycle (Q1 or Q4), in 90-day check-ins, or when addressing performance concerns. Use to structure feedback conversations, synthesize multi-rater input, and ensure consistency across the organization.

## When NOT to use
Skip if the employee is in their first 90 days (use check-in framework instead). Skip if performance concerns exist but no documented coaching has occurred. Skip if ratings are pre-determined by politics rather than evidence.

## Performance Review Framework

**Evidence-Based Assessment**

Gather data across 4 dimensions:
1. **Impact:** Delivered results against agreed-upon goals (OKRs, projects, metrics)
2. **Execution:** Quality of work, timeliness, problem-solving approach
3. **Growth:** Skill development, learning velocity, willingness to stretch
4. **Collaboration:** Teamwork, communication, cultural fit, leadership (if applicable)

**Rating Scale**
- **Exceeds Expectations:** Consistently delivers beyond goals; demonstrates mastery; elevates team
- **Meets Expectations:** Delivers on commitments; solid performance; role-appropriate
- **Needs Improvement:** Performance below role expectations; coaching and support needed; clear improvement plan required

**Rating Prohibitions**
- No "Meets" for someone who is underperforming
- No "Exceeds" without documented examples of above-and-beyond delivery
- No rating drift (bias) — benchmark against role expectations, not peer comparisons

## Review Structure

**Manager 1:1 Debrief (60 min)**
- Employee self-assessment (15 min): How did you perform against goals? Where did you stretch? Where did you fall short?
- Manager feedback (20 min): Impact on goals, execution quality, growth demonstrated, collaboration examples
- Forward-looking conversation (15 min): Skill gaps, development priorities, career aspirations, next-level readiness
- Rating discussion (5 min): Clear explanation of rating and reasoning
- Documentation: Key themes, rating, signature/date

**360 Feedback Collection (if applicable)**
- Collect feedback from 4–6 raters: manager, peers, direct reports (if applicable), cross-functional partners
- Standard template: 3–5 reflection questions on impact, teamwork, communication, growth
- Anonymized response collection (maintain confidentiality)
- Synthesis: Patterns, themes, consistent strengths and gaps

**Calibration Session (leadership team)**
- Compare ratings across similar roles and levels
- Identify rating drift or inconsistencies
- Ensure equity (no systematic bias by gender, ethnicity, tenure, or background)
- Lock ratings before communicating to employees

## Fairness Checkpoints

1. **Evidence-based:** Every claim tied to specific, documented example
2. **Role-aligned:** Expectations clear and achievable; not moving goalposts mid-cycle
3. **Equitable:** No systematic bias; rates calibrated against same standard across organization
4. **Developmental:** Even "Needs Improvement" includes clear path to success and manager support
5. **Legal defensibility:** Documented feedback, improvement plans, and consistent application
