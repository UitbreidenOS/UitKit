# CTA (Call-to-Action) Templates

Every newsletter needs ONE clear CTA. Use these templates to craft effective, actionable calls—then embed them naturally in your Takeaway section.

---

## CTA Types

### 1. Reply-Based CTAs

**Best for:** Building community, direct engagement, collecting feedback.

**Templates:**

- "Reply with [specific ask]—I read every response."
- "What's your biggest [challenge]? Reply and let me know."
- "Forward this to [person/team] and share their thoughts."
- "Disagree? Reply. I want to hear the contrarian take."

**Examples:**

- "Reply with your biggest blocker on agentic workflows—I'm tracking real enterprise challenges."
- "What's your AI consolidation strategy? Reply—I'm synthesizing responses for next week."
- "Forward this to your CTO. What do they think?"

**Why it works:** Direct engagement builds loyalty and gives you content ideas for future newsletters.

**Tracking:** Use email reply rate as metric. Target: 1–2% reply rate.

---

### 2. Link-Based CTAs

**Best for:** Driving traffic to your content, products, or partner resources.

**Templates:**

- "Read the full [resource] [link]"
- "Download the [report/playbook] [link]"
- "Register for [event] [link]"
- "Explore [product/service] [link]"
- "Check out [expert commentary] [link]"

**Examples:**

- "Read the full McKinsey report on enterprise AI [link]"
- "Download the AI consolidation playbook [link]"
- "Register for the agentic workflows masterclass [link]"

**Why it works:** Direct traffic and engagement metrics. Easy to track with UTM parameters.

**Tracking:** Use UTM params: `?utm_source=newsletter&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=[topic]`. Measure click-through rate (target: 3–7% of opens).

---

### 3. Subscription / Sign-Up CTAs

**Best for:** Growing your audience, expanding reach.

**Templates:**

- "Know someone who should read this? Share it [link]"
- "Subscribe for [frequency] deep dives [link]"
- "Join [X] other readers [link]"

**Examples:**

- "Know a PM or engineer who'd find this useful? Forward it."
- "Subscribe to get weekly deep dives on enterprise AI [link]"

**Why it works:** Low pressure, easy to share. Leverages word-of-mouth.

**Tracking:** Track forwards/shares via unique link. Measure new subscriber attribution.

---

### 4. Social CTA

**Best for:** Growing social presence, driving engagement on secondary channels.

**Templates:**

- "Continue this conversation on [platform] [link]"
- "What do you think? Share on [platform] [link]"
- "Follow for daily insights on [topic] [link]"

**Examples:**

- "Continue this debate on Twitter [link]—tag me."
- "Follow me on LinkedIn for daily AI consolidation updates [link]"

**Why it works:** Builds audience on platforms where you have influence.

**Tracking:** Use platform analytics to measure clicks and follows.

## Placement & Tone

### Natural Integration

**Weak (standalone, forced):**
"Please reply to this email."

**Strong (integrated into Takeaway):**
"If your company is consolidating AI vendors, reply with your approach—I'm tracking patterns and will share them in a future edition."

### Length

Keep CTAs to **1 sentence, 10–20 words max**. Anything longer feels like a sales pitch.

### Number of CTAs

**Use exactly 1 main CTA per newsletter.** Footer links (unsubscribe, social, archive) don't count.

- Too many CTAs = dilutes engagement, looks promotional
- One clear CTA = high conversion, professional tone

## Template Selection by Newsletter Type

| Newsletter Type | Best CTA | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Opinion / Commentary** | Reply-based | "Disagree? Reply." |
| **Trend Report** | Link-based | "Read the full report [link]" |
| **How-To / Guide** | Link-based | "Download the playbook [link]" |
| **Data Analysis** | Reply-based | "Share your take—reply." |
| **Community-Driven** | Reply-based | "What's your experience? Reply." |
| **Promotional** | Sign-up | "Subscribe for [frequency] [link]" |

## Examples by Context

### Enterprise AI Topic

**Weak CTA:** "Learn more about AI."

**Strong CTAs (pick 1):**

1. **Reply-based:** "What's your AI consolidation strategy? Reply—I'm tracking patterns across industries."

2. **Link-based:** "Download the consolidation playbook [link]."

3. **Social:** "Continue this on Twitter—[@you] [link]. What's your vendor stack look like?"

---

### Workflow Automation Topic

**Weak CTA:** "Check out our tool."

**Strong CTAs (pick 1):**

1. **Reply-based:** "Reply with one workflow you'd automate if you had infinite time and resources."

2. **Link-based:** "Read how [Company] cut deployment time by 60% [link]."

3. **Forward:** "Forward this to your engineering lead and ask their thoughts."

---

### Market Trend Topic

**Weak CTA:** "Read more."

**Strong CTAs (pick 1):**

1. **Reply-based:** "What do you think is next? Reply with your prediction."

2. **Link-based:** "Read the full research from [Organization] [link]."

3. **Social:** "Share your take on this trend [link]—tag me."

## Advanced: Multi-Step CTAs

**Use only if intentional.** Example:

"Reply with your biggest blocker (I'm reading every response). Then forward to your engineering lead and see what they say."

This works because:
- Primary CTA is clear: Reply
- Secondary CTA is a natural follow-up: Forward
- Both are optional and escalate engagement

## Tracking Your CTAs

### Metrics to Monitor

| CTA Type | Key Metric | Target |
|---|---|---|
| **Reply-based** | Reply rate (% of recipients who reply) | 1–2% |
| **Link-based** | Click-through rate (% of opens who click) | 3–7% |
| **Forward** | Forward rate (tracked via unique link) | Varies |
| **Social** | Clicks to social + engagement | Varies |

### A/B Testing CTAs

After 3–4 newsletters, try varying CTAs by segment:

- Send CTA A to 50% of list
- Send CTA B to 50% of list
- Measure reply rate, click rate, engagement
- Lock in the winner

Example test:

```
Newsletter: "AI Trends Q2 2026"

Segment A (50%): Reply-based CTA
"Reply with your vendor stack—curious what you're consolidating around."

Segment B (50%): Link-based CTA
"Download the consolidation playbook [link]"

Result: Reply CTA = 2.1% reply rate; Link CTA = 5.3% CTR
Decision: Use link CTAs for trend-focused topics, reply CTAs for opinion pieces
```

## Common Mistakes

**❌ Too many CTAs:**
"Reply here / Download here / Share on Twitter / Visit the site"

**✓ One clear CTA:**
"Reply with your AI vendor strategy—I'm tracking patterns."

---

**❌ Vague CTA:**
"Learn more about enterprise AI."

**✓ Specific CTA:**
"Read how Stripe consolidated their AI stack [link]."

---

**❌ Salesy CTA:**
"Sign up for our premium plan to unlock exclusive insights!"

**✓ Value-first CTA:**
"Subscribe for deep dives on enterprise trends [link]."

---

**❌ Hidden CTA:**
In the footer, buried.

**✓ Visible CTA:**
In the Takeaway section, clear and natural.

---

## Final Checklist

Before finalizing your CTA:

- [ ] It's exactly 1 primary CTA (footer links don't count)
- [ ] It's 1 sentence, 10–20 words
- [ ] It's specific, not vague ("Reply with X," not "Reply")
- [ ] It flows naturally from the Takeaway section
- [ ] You can track the metric (replies, clicks, forwards)
- [ ] It matches your newsletter type and audience
