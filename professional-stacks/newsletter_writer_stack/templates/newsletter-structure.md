# Newsletter Structure Template

Use this template as a starting point for every newsletter. Copy it, fill in sections, and then run through quality gates.

---

## Template

```
SUBJECT LINE: [Hook + data or urgency; max 50 characters]

PREVIEW TEXT: [Hook preview; 40–80 characters]

---

# [Newsletter Title / Week Number]

HOOK
[1–2 sentences that make the reader want to keep reading. Open with curiosity, a surprising statistic, or a bold take. No introductions.]

Example hooks:
- "Enterprises stopped chasing every AI announcement."
- "Three regulatory frameworks now govern 80% of global AI deployment."
- "The next wave of AI hiring has started—and it's not what you'd expect."

---

## CONTEXT: Why Now?

[100–150 words]

Answer: Why is this topic important right now? What changed? What event, trend, or data point triggered this newsletter?

Example:
"Six months into 2026, enterprise AI adoption has shifted. The early hype is gone. What remains: pragmatic, ROI-focused deployment. Leading companies are consolidating around what works, and the gap between winners and laggards is widening."

---

## INSIGHT 1: [Section Title]

[100–150 words]

Lead with specific data or expert take. Use:
- Statistics (with source)
- Case study (name + outcome)
- Expert quote (name, org)
- Trend analysis (what's changing and why)

Example:
"McKinsey's June 2026 survey found 67% of Fortune 500 companies are testing agentic workflows in production. 'We're past the talking phase,' says [Expert], CTO of [Company]. 'The bottleneck is now integration—getting agentic systems to talk to legacy infrastructure without breaking workflows.'"

---

## INSIGHT 2: [Section Title]

[100–150 words]

Continue the narrative. Each insight should:
- Build on the previous section
- Introduce a new angle or data point
- Connect to the reader's world

Example:
"Model consolidation is the real story. Companies are dumping multi-model stacks. Why? Cost. Latency. Complexity. The median enterprise was running 4.2 different LLM providers as of Q1; by Q2, that dropped to 2.1. A clear winner: single-vendor relationships with Anthropic and OpenAI are dominating the consolidation."

---

## INSIGHT 3 (optional): [Section Title]

[100–150 words]

If you have a third insight, add it here. Otherwise, move straight to Takeaway.

---

## TAKEAWAY: What This Means for You

[50–100 words]

Synthesize the insights. Answer: What should readers do with this knowledge?

- Don't tell them what to think.
- Tell them how to act.

Example:
"If your company is still evaluating multiple AI vendors, consolidation pressure is coming. Start testing agentic workflows with your single-vendor pick now. The gap between early movers and laggards will be unbridgeable by Q4."

---

## CTA

[1 sentence; 10–20 words]

Single, clear action. No link sprawl. Examples:

- "Reply with your biggest blocker—I'm tracking enterprise strategy shifts."
- "Download the full McKinsey report [link]."
- "Register for the webinar on agentic AI [link]."
- "Forward this to your engineering team and see what they think."

---

## FOOTER

[Standard footer—do not edit]

Have questions or feedback? [Reply to this email](mailto:you@example.com) or view past issues [here](link).

Follow us: [Twitter](link) · [LinkedIn](link) · [Your website](link)

© 2026 [Your Name]. [Unsubscribe](link) | [Preferences](link)

---
```

## Filling in the Template

### 1. Start with the hook
- Write 3 versions
- Pick the one that makes you want to keep reading
- Make sure it's specific, not generic

### 2. Research context
- Spend time here—readers will skip if the "why now" is weak
- Use recent event, data, or trend as anchor

### 3. Build insights
- Each insight = one clear idea
- Use data + human voice (quote, story, analysis)
- Make each section scannable (use subheadings)

### 4. Synthesize takeaway
- Don't re-summarize
- Tell readers how to act
- Be specific

### 5. Choose CTA
- One action only
- Make it easy to do (reply, click, forward)
- No buried CTAs—make it visible

### 6. Subject line
- Write 3 options after the body is done
- Test with `/optimize-subject-line`
- Pick the highest predicted open rate

## Word Count by Section

| Section | Word Count | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Hook** | 10–30 words | 1–2 short sentences |
| **Context** | 100–150 words | Set up the "why now" |
| **Insight 1** | 100–150 words | Lead with data |
| **Insight 2** | 100–150 words | Build the narrative |
| **Insight 3 (opt)** | 100–150 words | Only if needed |
| **Takeaway** | 50–100 words | Actionable conclusion |
| **CTA** | 10–20 words | Single action |
| **TOTAL BODY** | 300–800 words | Optimal: 500–600 |

---

## Quick Checklist Before Submit

- [ ] Hook is curiosity-driven, not generic
- [ ] Context answers "why now?"
- [ ] Each insight has data or expert voice
- [ ] Takeaway is actionable, not a summary
- [ ] CTA is single and clear
- [ ] No banned words
- [ ] Word count 300–800 (optimal 500–600)
- [ ] Subject line <50 chars
- [ ] Preview text 40–80 chars
- [ ] All links work
