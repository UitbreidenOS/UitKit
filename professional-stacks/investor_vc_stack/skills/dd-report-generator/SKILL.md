# DD Report Generator Skill

## When to activate

When partner requests a full due diligence report on a company that passed initial scoring (≥50).

## When NOT to use

- Companies scored PASS (<50) unless partner overrides with explicit justification
- Incomplete research (missing market analysis or financial data)
- Pre-revenue companies without clear path to validation

## Instructions

1. Gather all prior analysis: Opportunity score, market analysis, financial metrics, team background
2. Structure report as follows:

   **Executive Summary** (1 paragraph)
   - Company name, stage, TAM, key unit economics
   - Investment thesis fit (GO/REVIEW/PASS)
   - Red flags and mitigating factors
   - Recommendation in one sentence

   **Company Overview** (1 section)
   - Founding story, problem being solved
   - Team (founders + key hires, background)
   - Current traction (ARR, customer count, key metrics)

   **Market Opportunity** (1 section)
   - TAM/SAM with growth rate
   - Competitive landscape (top 3 competitors)
   - Company's differentiation and moat
   - Expansion opportunity

   **Financial Analysis** (1 section)
   - ARR/MRR, burn rate, runway
   - Unit economics (CAC, LTV, payback, gross margin)
   - Benchmark vs. stage
   - Red flags on unit economics

   **Risk Analysis** (1 section)
   - Founder/execution risk
   - Market risk (competition, TAM shrinkage)
   - Financial risk (runway, unit economics)
   - Operational risk (team gaps, key person)
   - Rated as High / Medium / Low for each

   **Investment Recommendation**
   - GO (invest), REVIEW (conditional on clarifications), or PASS
   - Rationale with 2–3 key reasons
   - Terms recommendation if applicable (check size, equity target)
   - Conditions for follow-on (milestones, metrics)

3. Format: Scannable with bold headers, tables for metrics, bullet points for risks
4. Length: 3–5 pages

## Example

**Report Title:** Due Diligence Report — [Company Name]

**Executive Summary**
XYZ Fintech is a Series B cross-border payment platform with $2M ARR and 20% MoM growth. Strong founding team with prior exits, clear unit economics (LTV:CAC 10x), and substantial TAM ($30B SMB payments, 12% CAGR). Primary risks: Competitive intensity from well-funded incumbents and 8-month runway pressure. Recommendation: GO — strong fundamentals and market opportunity outweigh near-term runway concern if Series C trajectory is clear.

[Sections as outlined above]
