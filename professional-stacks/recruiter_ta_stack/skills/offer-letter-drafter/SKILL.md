## When to activate

- Drafting offer letters for approved candidates
- Customizing offer templates for different roles and levels
- Including equity/stock option details and vesting schedules
- Ensuring legal compliance across jurisdictions
- Preparing competing offer counter-proposals

## When NOT to use

- For employment contracts with complex legal terms (consult legal)
- For international employment agreements (jurisdiction-specific)
- For contractor/freelance agreements

## Instructions

1. **Gather compensation data.** Base salary, bonus %, equity grant, signing bonus, and benefits summary.
2. **Select template.** Choose appropriate template: full-time, part-time, intern, or executive.
3. **Populate terms.** Start date, reporting manager, title, level, location/remote status, at-will statement.
4. **Detail equity.** Grant size, vesting schedule (standard 4yr/1yr cliff), exercise period, and current 409A valuation.
5. **Include benefits summary.** Health, 401k, PTO policy, learning budget, and any role-specific perks.
6. **Add legal clauses.** Confidentiality, IP assignment, non-solicitation (where enforceable), and dispute resolution.
7. **Review and personalize.** Add personal welcome note from hiring manager; verify all numbers match approved comp package.

## Example

```
Dear [Candidate],

We're thrilled to offer you the position of Senior Backend Engineer at [Company].

Base Salary: $165,000/year
Annual Bonus: 10% target ($16,500)
Equity: 5,000 options, 4-year vesting, 1-year cliff
Signing Bonus: $10,000
Start Date: July 1, 2026
Reports To: [Manager Name], VP Engineering

This offer expires on June 27, 2026. We can't wait to have you on the team!
```
