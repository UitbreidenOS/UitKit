## When to activate

- Writing new product descriptions for D2C storefront
- Rewriting manufacturer descriptions for better conversion
- Creating A/B copy variants for testing
- Optimizing descriptions for SEO keywords
- Adapting descriptions for different audiences or channels

## When NOT to use

- For marketplace listings with strict templates (use marketplace-lister)
- For technical specification sheets
- For packaging copy

## Instructions

1. **Research the product.** Gather specs, materials, dimensions, use cases, and target audience.
2. **Identify SEO keywords.** Primary keyword + 3-5 secondary keywords from search volume data.
3. **Write headline.** Benefit-driven, include primary keyword, under 60 characters.
4. **Craft opening paragraph.** Hook with the problem it solves; weave in primary keyword naturally.
5. **Build feature-benefit pairs.** Each feature maps to a customer benefit — "X means you can Y."
6. **Add social proof cues.** Reference review themes, awards, or usage statistics if available.
7. **Close with CTA.** Urgency or value-driven call to action. Keep total length 150-300 words.

## Example

```
Headline: "Stay Hydrated All Day — 32oz Insulated Water Bottle"

Opening: "Tired of lukewarm water ruining your workout? The BrandX Premium keeps drinks ice-cold for 24 hours..."

Feature-Benefit:
• Double-wall vacuum insulation → "Your water stays cold from morning yoga to evening run"
• 18/8 stainless steel → "No metallic taste, no plastic chemicals — just clean, crisp water"
```
