# Compensation Strategist

## When to activate

Annual merit cycle, new hire offer negotiation, market study required, or pay equity audit triggered. Requires compensation benchmarking data (Radford, Mercer, Levels.fyi) and current payroll data.

## When NOT to use

Not for severance negotiation (escalate to counsel). Not without board/CFO approval for major changes. Not for individual negotiation without market data.

## Compensation Framework

### Market Benchmarking
- **Data sources:** Radford for public/Series C+, Mercer for smaller companies, Levels.fyi/Blind for tech roles, Bureau of Labor Statistics
- **Positioning:** Target 50th percentile (median) for stable talent, 60th percentile for high-demand roles, 75th for critical leaders
- **Geographic adjustment:** SF/NYC 20–30% premium; remote roles at target location or negotiated blend
- **Tenure adjustment:** Entry-level premium 0%, mid-career +5–10%, senior +15–25%
- **Benchmark timing:** Annual refresh (market data updates quarterly); track comp creep

### Merit Increase Strategy
- **Performance-based:** Exceeds Expectations 4–6%, Meets Expectations 2–3%, Below Expectations 0–1%
- **Market adjustments:** If role market rate increases >5%, consider catch-up adjustment
- **Promotion merit:** Assume 10–15% increase for promotion to next level
- **Equity refresh:** Annual refresh at 20–30% of new-hire grant size; 4-year vest, 1-year cliff
- **Budget ceiling:** Merit pool typically 2–4% of payroll; negotiate with CFO/board

### Pay Equity Analysis
- **Methodology:** Regression analysis controlling for role, level, tenure, performance, location
- **Threshold:** Flag gaps >5% within same role/level/location for investigation
- **Outliers:** Identify and remediate (backfill with raises, or document business justification)
- **Transparency:** Communicate findings to leadership; plan corrective action
- **Audit schedule:** Annual review or post-acquisition/restructuring

### Offer Strategy
- **Salary band:** Role minimum, midpoint (market 50th), maximum (75th percentile)
- **Candidate positioning:** Entry-level (min), experienced external (midpoint), in-demand (max)
- **Negotiation anchor:** Lead with market data; justify with experience/fit
- **Equity:** Standard grant (e.g., 0.1–0.3% for mid-level IC) based on round/stage
