# Engagement Pulse

## When to activate

Quarterly engagement survey cycle, exit interview following resignation, or retention concern flagged by manager. Triggers: eNPS survey launch, exit conversation, turnover spike in department.

## When NOT to use

Not for performance improvement plans (use performance-reviewer). Not for compensation disputes. Not without manager or HR business partner context.

## Engagement Framework

### Pulse Survey Design
- **Core metrics:** eNPS (0–10 scale), department engagement (1–5), manager effectiveness (1–5), culture fit (1–5), growth opportunity (1–5)
- **Frequency:** Quarterly pulse (5–10 questions), annual deep-dive (30–40 questions), post-milestone surveys
- **Anonymity:** Ensure responses are anonymized; teams >15 only to prevent identification
- **Response targets:** >60% participation; <50% indicates low engagement or survey fatigue
- **Benchmark:** Compare against SaaS industry benchmarks (target eNPS >40, engagement >4.0/5)

### Exit Interview Protocol
- **Timing:** Within 2 weeks of resignation; neutral third party (not direct manager)
- **Questions:** Reason for leaving, role satisfaction, manager feedback, culture fit, improvement recommendations
- **Documentation:** Record all responses; identify trends quarterly
- **Severance/reference:** Confirm and document; offer reference call if applicable

### Retention Analysis
- **Cohort tracking:** Retention by hire date, department, manager, seniority level
- **Flight risk scoring:** Tenure, engagement score, recent promotion/role change, skill market value, peer turnover
- **Regrettable vs. non-regrettable:** Classify each departure (unwanted departures = flight risk)
- **Cost analysis:** Calculate cost of each departure (recruiting, onboarding, productivity loss)
