# Talent Acquisition Strategist

## When to activate

Job requisition approved by leadership, budget allocated, hiring timeline defined, and hiring manager available. Requires documented business case, compensation band, and headcount approval.

## When NOT to use

Not for contract/vendor hiring, internal transfers, or executive search (external recruiter). Not without business rationale and budget approval. Not for roles with unclear requirements or reporting structure.

## Hiring Strategy Framework

### Role Rationalization
- Strategic fit: How does this role advance company objectives?
- Headcount efficiency: Organic growth or could be absorbed by current team?
- Timeline: When must this role start? What dependencies exist?
- Market data: Salary competitiveness, skills scarcity, talent availability
- Bench impact: Which internal candidates could grow into this role long-term?

### Job Description Structure
- **Title:** Clear, searchable, salary-adjacent (e.g., "Senior Software Engineer, Backend — $160–190K")
- **Reporting:** Direct manager name/title, organization level
- **Overview:** 2–3 sentences on core mission and business impact
- **Responsibilities:** 5–7 action-verb bullets, measurable outcomes, not task-focused
- **Requirements:**
  - Must-haves (3–5): education, certifications, years of experience, critical skills
  - Nice-to-haves (3–5): preferred background, industry knowledge, tools
- **What we offer:** Culture, growth opportunities, equity, benefits overview
- **EEO statement:** Standard non-discrimination language, reasonable accommodations clause

### Sourcing Strategy
- **Direct channels:** LinkedIn recruiter, internal referrals (bonus structure), alumni networks
- **Job boards:** Industry-specific (Stack Overflow, Dribbble, Product Hunt), general (Angellist, Greenhouse)
- **Passive sourcing:** Company brand storytelling, employee networks, diversity platforms
- **Diversity sourcing:** Target 30%+ diverse interview slates; consider HBCU recruiting, women-in-tech groups
- **Agency option:** For executive or hard-to-fill roles (contingent fee structure)
- **Timeline:** Primary sourcing weeks 1–2, screening weeks 2–4, interviews weeks 3–6

### Interview Plan
Structured, bias-resistant process:
1. Recruiter Screener (30 min): Background, technical fit baseline, compensation expectations
2. Technical/Functional Assessment (60 min): Role-specific skills via case study, project portfolio, or coding exercise
3. Behavioral Interview (45 min): STAR-method questions on collaboration, conflict resolution, impact
4. Hiring Manager Round (60 min): Role-specific deep dive, team fit, growth discussion
5. Executive/Cross-functional Round (30 min): Strategic fit, culture alignment, final questions
Scoring: 1–5 per round; minimum total 18/25 to advance to offer

### Candidate Evaluation Rubric
- **Technical Fit (5 pts):** Skills depth, relevant experience, problem-solving ability, learning curve
- **Cultural Fit (5 pts):** Values alignment, communication style, collaboration approach, transparency
- **Growth Potential (5 pts):** Learning agility, ambition level, career trajectory, coachability
- **Team Dynamics (5 pts):** Peer feedback, manager chemistry, cross-functional synergy, mentorship fit
- **Cost Efficiency (5 pts):** Salary flexibility, equity comfort, negotiation posture, total comp alignment
**Total: 25 pts. Minimum 18 to move to offer.**

### Offer Strategy
- **Salary:** Market data-informed (50th percentile baseline), justified by experience and criticality
- **Equity:** 4-year vesting, 1-year cliff, annual refresh plan, exercise window transparency
- **Sign-on Bonus:** If hired from competitor or top-tier candidate (typically $10–50K)
- **Benefits:** Health insurance, 401(k), PTO, parental leave, professional development budget, equity
- **Start Date:** Flexibility within reasonable notice period (typically 2–4 weeks)
- **Reference Check:** Must pass; document all contacts, dates, and notes for audit trail

### 30-60-90 Onboarding Plan
- **Week 1:** IT/workspace setup, team intros, system orientation, first priority project assignment
- **Month 1:** Role immersion, direct report meetings, product/engineering deep dive, cross-functional intros
- **Month 2:** First project completion, feedback from manager and peers, development plan discussion
- **Month 3:** Performance review discussion, metrics check, growth trajectory planning, culture fit confirmation

## Example: Senior Product Manager, Growth

**Job Description Title:** Senior Product Manager, Growth — $140–170K + 0.15–0.25% equity

**Sourcing Channels:** LinkedIn recruiter (primary), internal referrals ($3K bonus), Angellist, Women in Product

**Interview Plan:**
1. Screener (30 min): Background, growth metrics experience, SaaS familiarity
2. Case Study (60 min): Design growth strategy for hypothetical B2B product scenario
3. Behavioral (45 min): STAR on cross-functional alignment, metric ownership, conflict resolution
4. Manager Round (60 min): Product roadmap discussion, framework methodology, leadership philosophy
5. Executive Round (30 min): Strategic fit, company vision alignment, final questions

**Candidate Rubric:**
- Technical: B2B SaaS experience, growth framework knowledge, metrics fluency (5 pts)
- Cultural: Ownership mindset, transparency, data-driven decision-making (5 pts)
- Growth: Strategic thinking, industry knowledge, coachability (5 pts)
- Team Dynamics: Manager synergy, peer feedback, communication clarity (5 pts)
- Cost: Salary alignment at 55th percentile, equity acceptance (5 pts)

**Success Metrics:** 8-week time-to-hire, 85% offer acceptance, 6-month retention >95%
