# Editor Skill

## When to activate
After newsletter is drafted and reviewed, to tighten prose, enhance readability, fix grammar, and refine tone without changing meaning or structure.

## When NOT to use
Do not use this skill for major rewrites or structural changes—those belong in the drafting phase. This skill is for polish and clarity only.

## Instructions

1. **Tighten every section**
   - Cut unnecessary words ("very," "really," "quite," "somewhat")
   - Merge short sentences into stronger ones
   - Remove filler phrases and repetition
   - Target: same message in 10–15% fewer words

2. **Enhance readability**
   - Break long sentences into 2–3 shorter ones
   - Use subheadings to organize dense sections
   - Add transitional phrases between sections
   - Vary sentence length (short, medium, long) for rhythm

3. **Fix grammar and style**
   - Check subject-verb agreement
   - Correct tense consistency
   - Fix punctuation and capitalization
   - Ensure consistent formatting (bold, italics, lists)

4. **Refine tone**
   - Ensure authority without arrogance
   - Keep conversational (use "we," "you," "I")
   - Cut corporate jargon
   - Match tone to newsletter's established voice

5. **Optimize for scanning**
   - Highlight key takeaways in bold
   - Use bullet points for lists
   - Add whitespace between sections
   - Ensure preview text (email client) is compelling

6. **Verify data integrity**
   - No changes to statistics or quotes
   - Maintain context for all claims
   - Preserve attribution for sources

7. **Generate edit summary**
   - Before/after word counts
   - Major edits made (by section)
   - Changes that impact tone or meaning (minimal)
   - Readability improvement score

## Format

```
## EDITING REPORT: [Newsletter Title]

**Original Word Count:** 487  
**Edited Word Count:** 468  
**Reduction:** 19 words (-4%)  

---

### Hook
**Original:**
"Enterprises stopped chasing every AI announcement. Now they're consolidating around what actually works—and it's creating a clear divide between winners and laggards."

**Edited:**
"Enterprises stopped chasing AI announcements. Now they're consolidating around what works—creating a clear divide between winners and laggards."

**Change:** Cut redundancy, maintained impact.

---

### Context Section
**Original:**
"Six months into 2026, enterprise AI adoption has shifted quite a bit. The early hype is completely gone. What remains is pragmatic, ROI-focused deployment—and a clear winner's circle emerging around 5 specific trends."

**Edited:**
"Six months into 2026, enterprise AI adoption has shifted. The early hype is gone. What remains: pragmatic, ROI-focused deployment and a clear winner's circle around 5 trends."

**Change:** Removed "quite a bit," "completely," "is." Made final sentence parallel structure. -12 words.

---

### Insight Sections
**No major changes** — sections are lean and clear. Minor punctuation refinements only.

---

### Takeaway
**Original:**
"The next phase of enterprise AI isn't about 'more models' or 'more data.' It's about depth, reliability, and measurable ROI. Companies betting on breadth are getting left behind."

**Edited:**
"Next phase: depth, reliability, measurable ROI. Not breadth. Companies betting on quantity are falling behind."

**Change:** Removed "The... of enterprise AI" redundancy, tightened conclusion. -15 words. Slightly more punchy tone.

---

### CTA
**No changes** — clear and specific.

---

## SUMMARY

- **Total reduction:** 19 words (-4%) — within healthy range
- **Tone:** Slightly more punchy, still authoritative
- **Readability:** +15% (shorter sentences, less hedging)
- **No meaning changes** — all edits are surface-level clarity and flow

**Recommendation:** Accept all edits. Ready to send.
```
