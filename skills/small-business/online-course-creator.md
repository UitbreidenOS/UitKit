# Online Course Creator

## When to activate
- You're an independent course creator (educator, expert, coach turning expertise into a course) and you're building or relaunching a course
- The curriculum is in your head and you need it structured into modules, lessons, and a teachable sequence
- You're about to launch and need the sales page, the email sequence, the social campaign, and the post-launch nurture flow
- An existing course needs a refresh — the curriculum is fine, but the marketing copy is dated and conversion is sliding
- You're considering whether to build a course at all and want to pressure-test the idea against your audience and market

## When NOT to use
- You're a beginner with no audience and no teaching experience — Claude can help with structure, but the core problem (no audience) needs to be solved first
- You're selling a regulated topic (medical, legal, financial advice) where outcome claims have legal exposure that requires human compliance review
- You sell premium 1:1 services and a course would cannibalize your higher-margin practice — solve the strategic question before drafting marketing copy

## Instructions

### Step 1: Set up your course context

Say:

"I'm building a course called [title]. The outcome the student gets is [transformation, in plain language]. The student persona is [persona — current state, goal, what they've tried]. The course delivery is [self-paced video / live cohort / hybrid]. Pricing is [$X]. My differentiator from other courses in this space is [reason]."

### Step 2: Curriculum structure

Ask Claude to help you structure the curriculum into modules and lessons.

Say:

"Based on my course context, propose a module-level curriculum structure for a [4 / 6 / 8] week course. Each module should have a clear outcome the student can verbalize, a lesson breakdown, and a practical assignment or exercise. Flag any modules where the student needs prerequisite knowledge."

You'll get a draft curriculum tree. Read it carefully — Claude will sometimes over-modularize (eight modules when six is enough) or under-modularize the hardest part of the material. Edit aggressively.

### Step 3: Lesson summaries and outlines

For each lesson, get a structured outline:

Say:

"For [module N, lesson M], draft a lesson outline: (1) the hook — why this matters, (2) the core concept in one sentence, (3) 3-5 supporting points, (4) a worked example, (5) a common mistake or misconception, (6) a quick check or reflection prompt."

Use these as the scaffolding for your video recording or your written lesson. The outline is not the lesson — your delivery is the lesson. The outline saves the planning time around the delivery.

### Step 4: Sales page

The sales page is the highest-stakes piece of writing for a course launch. Most course creators write it in agony over multiple weeks.

Say:

"Draft a long-form sales page for [course]. Structure: (1) hero — outcome promise in 2 lines, (2) the problem in the student's own language, (3) why most attempts at the outcome fail, (4) the alternative — what your course does differently, (5) what's inside (curriculum overview), (6) who it's for, (7) who it's not for, (8) testimonials placeholder, (9) FAQ, (10) pricing and guarantee. Match my brand voice."

Edit hard. Sales page copy is where Claude's drafts most often miss — it tends to over-promise on outcomes and under-emphasize the work the student needs to do. Tone down the outcome language and beef up the "this is hard, here's the work, here's why it's worth it" framing.

### Step 5: Launch email sequence

A typical course launch needs 8-12 emails over 7-14 days.

Say:

"Draft a 10-email launch sequence for [course]. Day 1: open cart. Day 2: case story. Day 4: address the biggest objection. Day 6: bonus or live Q&A invitation. Day 8: case story #2. Day 9: final 48 hours. Day 10: closing day with deadline. Day 11: cart closed, here's what's next. Match my brand voice and reference the specific transformation the course delivers."

You get the full sequence. Edit each email for your voice. The structure saves the planning; the editing is where your specific judgment goes.

### Step 6: Post-launch nurture

After a launch, students need a structured onboarding and weekly nurture during the course.

Say:

"For [course] starting [date], draft: (1) the day-1 welcome email, (2) a week-by-week check-in template the cohort coach can personalize, (3) the mid-course re-engagement email for students who haven't logged in in 14 days, (4) the course completion email with a clear next step (1:1, advanced course, community)."

## Example

You're an executive coach turning your methodology into an online course. The course is called "Leading Without Authority" — a 6-week self-paced course for senior individual contributors moving into staff/principal roles where they need to influence without formal management authority. Pricing: $1,997. Your audience is 8,000 newsletter subscribers, with about 600 active warm readers.

You spend a Saturday morning with Claude.

In 60 minutes, you have:
- A 6-module curriculum: (1) the architecture of influence without authority, (2) the four conversations every senior IC needs to master, (3) building a peer coalition, (4) navigating up — your manager and their manager, (5) handling the hard cases, (6) sustaining influence over 18+ months.
- A lesson-level outline for all 24 lessons (4 lessons per module).
- A 2,400-word sales page draft.
- A 10-email launch sequence.
- A 6-week student nurture sequence with week-by-week check-in templates.

You spend the next two weekends recording video lessons against the outlines. The marketing assets are 90% ready; you edit them for voice over two evenings. Launch is in 6 weeks instead of the 4-month timeline most course creators take.

You launch to your newsletter on a Tuesday. By end of week, 47 students enroll at $1,997 — $93,859 in launch revenue. Of that, perhaps 15% is attributable to faster time-to-launch (you didn't lose interest readers during a long build) and another 15% to higher-quality copy than you would have written from a blank page. So roughly $28K of the launch revenue is directly attributable to the Claude workflow.

The course continues to sell at a lower volume after launch via evergreen email sequences. Compounding revenue across the next 12 months: another $180-220K.
