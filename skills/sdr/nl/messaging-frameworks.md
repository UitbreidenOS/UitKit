# Messaging Frameworks

## When to activate

Je bent aan het werken aan een koude outreach (e-mail, LinkedIn of call script) en je moet bepalen welk framework de antwoordsnelheid en conversatiekwaliteit voor een specifieke prospect maximaal maakt. Beslissingspunt: je hebt hun naam, functie, bedrijf en één signaal (trigger event, gedrag van collega's, industrietrend of indicator van pijnpunt).

## When NOT to use

- Nadat je al een respons hebt of een lopend gesprek voert — gebruik response frameworks in plaats daarvan
- Warme introducties of referral-driven outreach — gebruik relatiebouwende taal in plaats daarvan
- Prospecting naar accounts waar je helemaal geen signaal hebt en geen onderzoeksgegevens
- Outbound op grote schaal zonder eerst framework fit voor je ICP te testen
- Wanneer de prospect expliciet heeft afgemeld of je als spam heeft gemarkeerd

## Instructions

### Core Decision Logic

Kies een framework op basis van **signaalsterkte** en **type beslissingsmaker**:

| Signaalsterkte | Doelfunctie | Aanbevolen Framework |
|---|---|---|
| **Zeer sterk** (event, metric, dringende pijnpunt) | Elke level | **Do The Math** of **Short Trigger** |
| **Sterk** (concurrentie-inlichtingen, industrietrend) | C-suite / Director+ | **Challenge of Similar Companies** |
| **Gemiddeld** (algemeen industrieinsicht) | Director / VP | **Leader Responsibilities** |
| **Zwak of geen** | Nieuw contact, koude lijst | **Ask Before Pitch** of **Neutral Insight** |
| **Account met veel ruis** (veel koude e-mails) | Elke | **Pattern Interrupt** |
| **Lange verkoopscyclus** (12+ maanden) | Elke | **Upfront Value** |

### Framework 1: Do The Math

**Formula:** Specifiek getal → bedrijfsgevolg → dollar/groeibetekenis → vraag

**Structuur (3–4 zinnen):**
1. Begin met een concreet kengetal gekoppeld aan hun bedrijf ("Je 200-persoonsteam," "Je $50M ARR," "Je 10K+ maandelijkse leads")
2. Bereken impact ("Als slechts 10% X verbeterde, dat is $Y extra opbrengst")
3. Geef aan wat verbetering blokkeert (impliciet of expliciet)
4. Zacht verzoek voor gesprek

**Works best for:** Finance-minded buyers (CFOs, Finance Directors, COOs), competitive industries (SaaS, logistics, retail), performance-obsessed cultures

**Trigger signals:** Aangekondigd financieringsronde, aanwervingsspurt, omzetaankondiging, openbare prestatiemisser, concurrent financieringsronde

**Prompt template:**

```
Write a cold email using the "Do The Math" framework for [PROSPECT_NAME] at [COMPANY].

Context:
- [PROSPECT] is a [ROLE] at [COMPANY]
- [COMPANY] has [SIZE: team / ARR / customers]
- Problem area: [SPECIFIC BUSINESS METRIC THEY CARE ABOUT, e.g., "sales rep productivity," "customer churn," "deal cycle length"]
- Our solution impacts: [METRIC IMPROVEMENT, e.g., "rep ramp time," "win rate," "forecast accuracy"]

Math to highlight:
- Baseline: [CURRENT STATE, e.g., "Your 200 reps average 6 months to full quota"]
- Improvement scenario: [REALISTIC UPLIFT, e.g., "Industry leaders hit 4 months"]
- Value at stake: [CALCULATION, e.g., "30 fewer rep-months of ramping = $1.2M saved annually"]

Write a subject line + 4-sentence email body (under 90 words). Use the framework:
1. Name the metric and their specific number
2. Show the gap/opportunity (our insight)
3. Quantify the cost of status quo
4. Propose a 15-min conversation to explore fit

Tone: confident, specific, zero fluff.
```

**Example:**

**Subject:** 30 reps missing quota at [COMPANY]

Your 200-rep field team likely has 30–40 reps missing quota this quarter—that's $2.4M in lost pipeline at your ASP. We analyzed why: most teams don't hit month 1 targets in the first 90 days, extending ramp to 6 months instead of 4.

We help 3 companies in [VERTICAL] compress ramp to 4 months. Over two years, that's $3.6M in recovered revenue per cohort.

Worth a 15-min call to see if we're a fit?

—[Your name]

### Framework 2: Short Trigger

**Formula:** Event identified + immediate implication + specific ask (3 lines max, <60 words)

**Structuur:**
1. Noem een recent, controleerbare event (aanwerving, productlancering, financiering, integratie, functieberte, marktbeweging)
2. Geef het ene wat telt als gevolg
3. Vraag 15–20 minuten om te onderzoeken

**Works best for:** Mid-market and enterprise, any role (triggers bypass seniority), transactional sales cycles (3–6 months)

**Trigger signals:** IPO, financieringsaankondiging, verandering in bestuur, nieuwe CMO/CRO-aanwerving, productlancering, earnings call mention, uitgebreide voetafdruk in nieuwe geo

**Prompt template:**

```
Write a cold email using the "Short Trigger" framework for [PROSPECT_NAME] at [COMPANY].

Trigger event: [SPECIFIC, RECENT, VERIFIABLE EVENT]
Implication for [PROSPECT_NAME]'s role: [ONE CONSEQUENCE THEY OWN]
Our value: [HOW WE REDUCE FRICTION / ACCELERATE / MITIGATE THAT ONE CONSEQUENCE]

Write a subject line + exactly 3 sentences (max 60 words total):
1. Acknowledge the trigger event (shows research, not spray & pray)
2. Name the implication for their specific function
3. Ask for 15 min to discuss fit

Tone: timely, direct, zero context needed.
```

**Example:**

**Subject:** Re: [COMPANY]'s new product launch

Congrats on the Q2 launch of [PRODUCT]. You'll onboard ~500 new customers in 60 days—but onboarding delays are the #1 churn driver at your scale. We cut time-to-value by 3 weeks for similar launches at [PEER COMPANY].

15 min call worth it?

—[Your name]

### Framework 3: Challenge of Similar Companies

**Formula:** Peer company doing X → your target still on old way → competitive gap + implication

**Structuur (4 zinnen):**
1. Noem 1–2 competitieve/peer bedrijven die iets slims doen
2. Beschrijf wat ze doen (specifiek, niet vaag)
3. Contrast met huidige aanpak van doel (status quo)
4. Impliceer concurrentienadeel of gemiste kans
5. Vraag om chat

**Works best for:** Directors, VPs, Chief Revenue Officers, competitive markets (SaaS, B2B services, fintech)

**Trigger signals:** Analystrapport dat concurrenten noemt, industrieconferentie, earnings call die concurrentieduk noemt, artikel over concurrentinnovatie

**Prompt template:**

```
Write a cold email using the "Challenge of Similar Companies" framework for [PROSPECT_NAME] at [COMPANY].

Competitive context:
- Peer company winning in your space: [COMPANY_A]
- What they're doing: [SPECIFIC APPROACH / TACTIC / TECHNOLOGY]
- Business outcome: [RESULT: faster sales, higher ACVs, better retention, etc.]
- [COMPANY]'s current approach: [STATUS QUO OR OUTDATED METHOD]
- Implied gap: [CONSEQUENCE: slower sales, lower win rate, churn risk, etc.]

Write a subject line + 4-sentence email body:
1. Acknowledge their market position
2. Reference what competitors are doing (cite if possible, e.g., "recent earnings call," "industry report")
3. Describe the gap between their approach and the new standard
4. Imply competitive risk without being alarmist
5. Ask for 20 min to explore how others are closing the gap

Tone: peer-to-peer insight, not FUD. Specific, not generic.
```

**Example:**

**Subject:** How [PEER_1] and [PEER_2] are closing the gap on [COMPANY]

I've noticed [PEER_1] recently deployed [TACTIC] in their [REGION] sales org—and their quota attainment jumped 18%. [PEER_2] followed suit Q1. Most teams in your space are still running [OLD METHOD].

That's a material gap. Worth 20 min to see what the leaders are doing differently?

—[Your name]

### Framework 4: Neutral Insight

**Formula:** Counterintuitive data point + zero agenda + implicit relevance + ask

**Structuur (3–4 zinnen):**
1. Begin met een bevinding of trend die verrassend is of gangbare wijsheid tegenspreekt
2. Geef bron / geloofwaardigheid (analystrapport, enquête, openbare gegevens)
3. Geef geen conclusie — laat hen die trekken
4. Zacht verzoek voor context exchange

**Works best for:** Top-of-funnel, brand awareness, long-cycle deals (9–18 months), any seniority level, trust-building

**Trigger signals:** Industrie onderzoeksvrij, analystrapport, nieuwe marktgegevens, trendverschuiving

**Prompt template:**

```
Write a cold email using the "Neutral Insight" framework for [PROSPECT_NAME] at [COMPANY].

Insight:
- Data point or finding: [COUNTERINTUITIVE FACT]
- Source: [ANALYST REPORT / SURVEY / PUBLIC DATA / RESEARCH]
- Relevance to [PROSPECT_NAME]'s industry/role: [IMPLIED, NOT STATED]

Write a subject line + 3-sentence email body:
1. Lead with the finding (no context, just the surprise)
2. Cite the source credibly
3. Note relevance to their [FUNCTION / VERTICAL / MARKET]
4. Ask if they've seen similar patterns; offer to share full report/source

Tone: curious, non-salesy, collaborative. You're sharing, not selling.
```

**Example:**

**Subject:** Interesting finding from the Gartner CRO survey

Saw this in the 2026 Gartner CRO Survey: companies running async training vs. live ramp actually hit quota 2 months *faster*. Counterintuitive, because most orgs default to in-person onboarding.

Curious if you're seeing anything similar in [VERTICAL]. Happy to share the full report.

—[Your name]

### Framework 5: Leader Responsibilities

**Formula:** Noem wat ze op gemeten worden → identificeer risico/kans in die metric → position oplossing als hefboom

**Structuur (4 zinnen):**
1. Identificeer hun titel en kernKPI (pipeline, CAC, churn, win rate, quota attainment, forecast accuracy)
2. Breng een trend of gat in die KPI aan het licht
3. Position je oplossing als hefboom om het te verplaatsen
4. Vraag om context gesprek

**Works best for:** VPs, Directors, C-suite, sales/revenue-focused roles (CRO, VP Sales, VP Customer Success, CFO), high-ASP deals

**Trigger signals:** Analystrapport, earnings call, bestuursvergadering (afgeleid uit openbare info), teamuitbreiding in die functie

**Prompt template:**

```
Write a cold email using the "Leader Responsibilities" framework for [PROSPECT_NAME] at [COMPANY].

Prospect context:
- Title: [ROLE, e.g., "VP Sales," "Director of Customer Success"]
- Core KPI they own: [METRIC, e.g., "pipeline coverage," "CAC," "net retention," "forecast accuracy"]
- Industry/company size: [VERTICAL, SIZE]

Current state:
- Typical challenge for [ROLE] in [VERTICAL]: [SPECIFIC METRIC GAP, e.g., "CAC rising 12% YoY while LTV stagnates"]
- Trend in the industry: [CONTEXT, e.g., "top performers are shifting to X"]
- Implication: [CONSEQUENCE, e.g., "unit economics at risk if CAC isn't controlled"]

Our angle:
- Our solution directly improves [METRIC] by [HOW: e.g., "reducing sales cycle by 3 weeks"]

Write a subject line + 4-sentence email:
1. Name their title and the metric they own
2. Reference a trend or benchmark in that metric
3. Tie our solution to moving their KPI
4. Ask for 20 min to explore fit without pressure

Tone: respectful of their responsibility, specific to their function.
```

**Example:**

**Subject:** [COMPANY]'s CAC is likely up this quarter

As VP Sales at [COMPANY], you're measured on pipeline coverage and CAC efficiency. I was reviewing [VERTICAL] benchmarks—most teams' CAC is up 15–20% YoY while their ADR stayed flat. That's a margin squeeze.

We helped [PEER_COMPANY] (similar size/vertical) reduce sales cycle by 3 weeks, which dropped CAC by 18%. Worth 20 min to explore if the same lever works for your org?

—[Your name]

### Framework 6: Ask Before Pitch

**Formula:** Acknowledge coldness + ask for permission + zero commitment

**Structuur (2–3 zinnen):**
1. Begin met een directe verklaring dat je koud bereikt
2. Vraag of het onderwerp relevant is voordat je tijd investeert
3. Geef hen een uitweg zonder schuldgevoel

**Works best for:** Very cold, high-value accounts, C-suite (CEO, CFO, COO), technical founders, accounts with high noise/inbound

**Trigger signals:** No specific signal; used for virgin territory or very senior decision-makers where credibility/permission matters more than trigger

**Prompt template:**

```
Write a cold email using the "Ask Before Pitch" framework for [PROSPECT_NAME] at [COMPANY].

Context:
- Cold situation: You have little signal; prospect is high-value
- Their role: [TITLE]
- Your hypothesis: [ROUGH IDEA WHY THIS MIGHT MATTER TO THEM]

Write a subject line + 2-3 sentence email:
1. Start with honesty: acknowledge you're cold and reaching unsolicited
2. State your rough hypothesis (not a pitch, just a question)
3. Ask: "Before I waste your time, is this even in your orbit?"
4. Give them an easy out ("if not, no worries—appreciate you taking a look")

Tone: humble, respectful of their time, permission-seeking.
```

**Example:**

**Subject:** Quick permission question

I'm reaching cold here. I help SaaS founders optimize payroll tax and equity structure during scale—and I'm guessing that's either table stakes or completely off your plate right now.

Before I send anything else: is this even worth a 15-min conversation, or should I stop here?

No wrong answer. Appreciate it either way.

—[Your name]

### Framework 7: Upfront Value

**Formula:** Lead with a resource, insight, or help that benefits them regardless of buying → ask for context conversation after

**Structuur (3–4 zinnen):**
1. Identificeer een specifiek wrijvingspunt in hun rol/industrie
2. Bied een concrete resource zonder voorwaarden (sjabloon, script, gegevens, intro, analyse)
3. Zend het onmiddellijk (bijlage, link of aanbod om te sturen)
4. Vraag om kort context gesprek om te begrijpen of een bredere fit bestaat

**Works best for:** Long sales cycles (12+ months), intent-to-build trust, any seniority, relationship-focused deals, vertical expertise plays

**Trigger signals:** Industrie trend die nieuwe tooling vereist, rolfunctie overgang (nieuw in dienst), openbare uitbreiding, teamschaling

**Prompt template:**

```
Write a cold email using the "Upfront Value" framework for [PROSPECT_NAME] at [COMPANY].

Value to offer:
- Friction point in [PROSPECT_NAME]'s role: [SPECIFIC PAIN, e.g., "hiring top sales engineers is hard; there's no standard scorecard"]
- Resource you can provide: [CONCRETE DELIVERABLE, e.g., "hiring scorecard template we use with 150+ hiring managers," "competitive analysis comparing your stack to 20 similar-sized SaaS companies," "30-min intro to a CRO who just solved a similar problem"]
- Delivery method: [HOW YOU'LL SHARE IT: attachment, link, intro, or brief conversation]

Write a subject line + 3-sentence email:
1. Name the friction and your observation
2. Offer the resource (be specific: template, data, intro, analysis)
3. Deliver it or offer to send with no strings
4. Ask for 15 min feedback on their situation so you understand fit for future

Tone: generosity first, sales second. You're helping before asking.
```

**Example:**

**Subject:** Sales engineering hiring scorecard (no pitch)

I noticed [COMPANY] recently hired 3 sales engineers. Hiring for that role is brutal—no standard scorecard, high false positives. We've developed a hiring rubric used by 150+ hiring managers across [VERTICAL]; it cuts evaluation time by 60% and improves long-term retention by 18%.

Attached. No strings. Happy to send the 6-month outcomes data if useful.

If you've got 15 min in the next week, I'd love to hear how you're building the team—might be useful for future conversations.

—[Your name]

### Framework 8: Pattern Interrupt

**Formula:** Break the expected cold-email script with something unexpected → grab attention → ask for response

**Structuur (1–2 zinnen):**
1. Begin met iets atyisch, polariserend of amusant dat het generieke koude-email template tegenspreekt
2. Noem wat je eigenlijk wilt (meestal gewoon een respons of gesprek)
3. Houd het kort; nieuwsgierigheid kloof is de haak

**Works best for:** High-volume cold targets (inbound-heavy companies, tech founders, investors), accounts you're tier 2 on, brand awareness, list environments (not precision targeting)

**Trigger signals:** No specific signal; used when volume matters more than precision, or when target receives high cold-email volume

**Prompt template:**

```
Write a cold email using the "Pattern Interrupt" framework for [PROSPECT_NAME] at [COMPANY].

Context:
- Target receives a lot of cold outreach (yes/no)
- Your goal: get a response, not a deal this email
- Angle: [UNUSUAL OBSERVATION ABOUT THEM, THEIR COMPANY, THEIR MARKET, OR A CONTRARIAN TAKE]

Write a subject line + 1-2 sentence email body:
1. Open with something unexpected: a contrarian take, a funny observation, a surprising data point, or an honest admission
2. State exactly what you want (a response, a 10-min call, their opinion on X)
3. Keep it conversational and short

Tone: irreverent, honest, memorable. The goal is curiosity, not credibility.
```

**Example:**

**Subject:** You're doing the opposite of what your competitors are doing (in a good way)

Most [VERTICAL] companies I talk to are building for enterprise. You're clearly going upmarket. That's either genius or a costly mistake—I'm genuinely curious which.

Honest take: what's your thesis?

—[Your name]

### Decision Tree: Which Framework to Use

```
START: Cold outreach for [PROSPECT] at [COMPANY]

1. Do I have a strong, recent signal? (event, metric, announcement)
   YES → Short Trigger or Do The Math
   NO → Continue to Q2

2. Is this a very high-value account with no signals?
   YES → Ask Before Pitch
   NO → Continue to Q3

3. Do I have competitive intel or peer behavior data?
   YES → Challenge of Similar Companies
   NO → Continue to Q4

4. Is this a long-cycle deal (12+ months) or trust-building play?
   YES → Neutral Insight or Upfront Value
   NO → Continue to Q5

5. Am I targeting a titled leader (Director+) on a KPI?
   YES → Leader Responsibilities
   NO → Continue to Q6

6. Does the target receive lots of cold outreach (high noise)?
   YES → Pattern Interrupt
   NO → Neutral Insight (default for warm audiences)
```

### Benchmarks & Success Metrics

| Framework | Typical Reply Rate | Best Use | Cycle Impact |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Do The Math** | 8–12% | Strong economic signal, quota-driven orgs | Shortens by 2–3 weeks (instant credibility) |
| **Short Trigger** | 12–18% | Event-driven, transactional buyers | Fastest time-to-response (3–5 days) |
| **Challenge of Similar Companies** | 6–10% | Competitive FOMO, Director/VP level | Moves to decision faster if concerns exist |
| **Neutral Insight** | 4–6% | Top-of-funnel, brand building | Longest—trust-building play (3–6 months) |
| **Leader Responsibilities** | 8–14% | KPI-driven leaders, enterprise | Moves deal forward if pain acute |
| **Ask Before Pitch** | 5–9% | Ultra-high-value, C-suite | High "no thanks" rate but high-quality responders |
| **Upfront Value** | 7–11% | Long-cycle, relationship-heavy | Establishes credibility for future outreach |
| **Pattern Interrupt** | 3–8% | Volume plays, brand awareness | High novelty, low conversion but low cost |

## Example

### Scenario: SDR at B2B SaaS prospecting a Series B fintech company

**Context:**
- **Target:** Sarah Chen, VP Sales at PayFlow (Series B fintech, $15M ARR, 60-person sales team)
- **Signal:** PayFlow just announced a $35M Series B and is expanding into EMEA (public news)
- **Your solution:** Sales coaching AI that accelerates ramp time
- **Current approach:** Generic cold email (2% reply rate)

**Decision:**
1. Strong signal ✓ (funding + expansion = hiring spree)
2. Role: VP Sales ✓ (KPI-driven, measured on quota attainment)
3. Vertical: fintech (competitive, metrics-obsessed) ✓
4. Choose: **Do The Math** + **Short Trigger** hybrid

**Framework Selection Logic:**
- The funding is the trigger event (Short Trigger domain)
- But Sarah, as VP Sales, cares most about ramping new reps into EMEA market (Do The Math domain)
- Hybrid angle: "Your EMEA expansion will hire 20 reps—if they hit month 2 targets, you'll book $2.1M extra ARR. If they slip, you're down $2.1M. That's the ramp problem."

**Email Output:**

---

**Subject:** 20 reps, 4 months, $2.1M swing

Sarah—congrats on the $35M Series B. With EMEA expansion, you're hiring 20 new reps in H2. Here's the math: if they hit month 2 targets, your Q4 bookings spike $2.1M. If ramp slips to 5 months (normal), you're down $2.1M in pipeline.

Most Series B teams lose that swing to slow ramp. We've cut ramp time from 5 to 3 months for 8 fintech teams at your stage—giving them back the $2M in early-quarter productivity.

Worth 15 min to see if your EMEA hiring plan could use the same acceleration?

[Your name]

---

**Why this works:**
- **Trigger:** Public funding news (shows research, credibility)
- **Math:** Specific to her expansion (60 sales team × 20 new reps = $2.1M swing tied to her KPI)
- **Proof:** Vertical-specific proof point (8 fintech teams, not generic)
- **Ask:** Soft, time-bound, low friction
- **Expected result:** 10–14% reply rate (Short Trigger + Do The Math combo), with 60%+ qualified interest rate

### When to Test

A/B test frameworks at 50-email cohorts:
- **Weeks 1–2:** Pick one framework, send to 50 prospects in same segment
- **Measure:** Reply rate, reply quality (qualified vs. noise), time-to-first-response
- **Iterate:** If reply rate <5%, switch framework; if >10%, double down
- **Lock in:** Once one framework hits 10%+ reply rate in your ICP, use as default; test others in lower-priority segments
