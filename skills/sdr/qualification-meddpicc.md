# MEDDPICC Qualification

## When to activate

- SDR has completed initial qualification call and is determining whether to schedule an AE (sales engineer / account executive) discovery meeting
- You need to assess deal quality across all eight MEDDPICC dimensions before handoff
- Evaluating whether to continue pursuing a prospect when deal signals are mixed
- Building a handoff brief for the AE with documented evidence across all qualification elements

## When NOT to use

- Use this after initial contact but before AE engagement — not for post-discovery or existing accounts
- Do not use this for inbound leads that have already passed initial filter — use this to decide *whether* to engage AE, not to validate AE involvement
- Not a replacement for active listening; use this to structure findings after the conversation, not during
- Do not force-fit marginal leads through MEDDPICC scoring — if core elements are Unknown/Missing, that signals more discovery needed, not readiness for handoff

## Instructions

### MEDDPICC Framework Definition

**M — Metrics**: Quantified business impact. Ask: "If you solve this problem, what does that mean in revenue, cost savings, time saved, or risk reduction? What's the financial or operational impact?"
- Strong: Prospect articulated specific numbers ($X savings, Y% efficiency gain, Z revenue impact)
- Partial: Prospect indicated impact but not quantified ("save money," "faster," "reduce errors")
- Unknown: You asked but prospect couldn't quantify
- Missing: No conversation about impact yet

**E — Economic Buyer**: Who controls budget? Have you spoken to them or only an influencer?
- Strong: You have confirmed conversation with person who owns budget authority
- Partial: Prospect confirmed who Economic Buyer is but you haven't spoken directly; budget authority is likely
- Unknown: Unclear who controls budget; prospect is deflecting
- Missing: No conversation about budget ownership

**D — Decision Criteria** (first D): What are the top 3–5 evaluation criteria the prospect will use to judge vendors?
- Strong: Prospect explicitly named 3+ criteria in priority order (e.g., "1. integrates with Salesforce, 2. under $50k/year, 3. 24/7 support")
- Partial: Prospect mentioned 1–2 criteria but not comprehensive or prioritized
- Unknown: You asked but prospect was vague ("we want the best solution")
- Missing: No conversation about how they'll evaluate

**D — Decision Process** (second D): Who is in the buying committee? What are the steps? What's the timeline?
- Strong: Prospect mapped out committee (founder, CFO, ops lead), process (RFP → demo → security review → sign-off), and timeline (4–6 weeks)
- Partial: Prospect identified some stakeholders and rough timeline but process is unclear
- Unknown: Prospect unclear on steps or who else is involved
- Missing: No conversation about buying process

**P — Paper Process**: Procurement, legal, security review, compliance sign-offs — who owns each and how long?
- Strong: Prospect identified every gate (e.g., "security review takes 2 weeks, procurement is 1 week, legal is fast")
- Partial: Prospect acknowledged some gates exist but not clear on owners or timelines
- Unknown: Prospect unsure about procurement / legal / security rigor
- Missing: No conversation about sign-off gates

**I — Identify Pain**: What is the specific, quantified pain? What happens if they do nothing?
- Strong: Prospect articulated specific, quantified pain ("We lose 8 hours/person/week on manual data entry") and consequence of inaction ("We'll miss Q3 forecast")
- Partial: Prospect mentioned pain but not specific or quantified ("It's a headache")
- Unknown: Prospect acknowledged a problem but won't quantify or articulate consequence
- Missing: No discussion of pain or consequence

**C — Champion** (first C): Do you have someone internally selling for you when you're not in the room?
- Strong: Identified person who will evangelize, has stated commitment, has skin in the game (e.g., their workflow improves)
- Partial: Identified likely champion but commitment unclear or they have mixed incentives
- Unknown: Prospect suggested a possible champion but you haven't validated
- Missing: No champion identified; everyone is neutral or skeptical

**C — Competition** (second C): Which vendors are they evaluating? What do they love/hate about those options?
- Strong: Prospect named 2+ competitors, stated what's good/bad about each vs. what they need
- Partial: Prospect mentioned 1 competitor or features they like/dislike but no competitive comparison
- Unknown: Prospect deflected or said "we haven't looked at alternatives"
- Missing: No conversation about competitive set

### MEDDPICC Scoring Logic

Score each element on a 0–3 scale:
- **3 = Strong**: All or nearly all information present, quantified, and confirmed by prospect directly
- **2 = Partial**: Some information present; may lack quantification, confirmation from decision-maker, or completeness
- **1 = Unknown**: You asked but got vague or evasive response
- **0 = Missing**: No conversation about this element or prospect explicitly said "we don't know yet"

**SAP Score (Sales Accuracy Predictor)**: Sum all eight elements. Maximum = 24.

Interpretation:
- **20–24 (Excellent)**: 6–8 elements at Strong; ready for AE handoff; high close probability
- **14–19 (Good)**: Mix of Strong and Partial; meets handoff threshold; AE should confirm Pain/Champion/Metrics in discovery
- **12–13 (Threshold Caution)**: More Partial than Strong; handoff marginal; escalate to manager if Economics/Decision Process are weak
- **0–11 (Not Ready)**: Too many Unknown/Missing; requires another discovery call before AE engagement; schedule follow-up

**Handoff Threshold**: SDR must achieve **14+ SAP Score** before scheduling AE discovery. Do not schedule AE for score < 14 without manager approval.

### Discovery Questions by MEDDPICC Element

Use these questions to probe each dimension during qualification call:

**Metrics:**
- "If you implement a solution to this problem, what does success look like in financial or operational terms? What's the impact in dollars, hours saved, or risk reduction?"
- "How are you measuring the cost of the problem today? What's the annual burden?"
- "What KPIs would change once this is solved?"

**Economic Buyer:**
- "Who owns the budget for a solution like this?"
- "Are you the right person to speak to about budget, or should we include [their name] on our next call?"
- "Who else would need to approve spend in your organization for something like this?"

**Decision Criteria:**
- "When you evaluate vendors, what are the top 3–5 things that matter most to you? In what order?"
- "What's non-negotiable vs. nice-to-have?"
- "If you had to choose between [price/integration/support], which wins?"

**Decision Process:**
- "Walk me through how a buying decision would unfold in your org. Who needs to sign off?"
- "Are there any formal steps like an RFP, demo, security review, or legal review?"
- "What's the typical timeline from first conversation to contract?"

**Paper Process:**
- "Do you have a procurement process? Who owns it and how long does it usually take?"
- "Is there a security review or compliance sign-off? Who handles it?"
- "Does legal review vendor contracts? How long does that take?"

**Identify Pain:**
- "What's the specific pain you're feeling today? Can you describe it in terms of what's broken or slowing you down?"
- "How often does this problem occur? How long does it take to work around it?"
- "What happens if you don't solve this by [target date]? What's the cost of doing nothing?"

**Champion:**
- "Who on your team will benefit most from solving this problem?"
- "Is there anyone in your org who's been pushing for a solution to this?"
- "Who would you naturally loop in when discussing a potential vendor?"

**Competition:**
- "Are you evaluating other solutions or vendors for this?"
- "What are they looking at? Have they demoed or piloted anything?"
- "What do you like or dislike about the options you're considering?"

### Common Pitfalls & How to Avoid Them

**Most Commonly Missed Element: Competition**

Most SDRs skip competitive discovery because:
- They fear it makes the prospect think "there are other options" (actually, most enterprises ARE evaluating multiple vendors)
- They don't know how to ask without sounding defensive
- They assume prospect will volunteer competitive info (they won't)

**Fix:**
- Ask early, frame as "understanding landscape": "Are you working with any other vendors on this right now?"
- Reframe as validation of process: "Most teams I talk to evaluate 2–3 options. Who else are you looking at?"
- Use prospect's answer to differentiate: "They're strong on [feature], but struggle with [feature we do well]. Is that a concern?"

**Other Common Gaps:**

- **Metrics Unknown**: Prospect says "save time" but won't quantify. Action: "If you save 1 hour/person/week, what's that worth in salary cost?" (prompt them, don't accept vague)
- **Economic Buyer Missing**: You've only spoken to an influencer. Action: Schedule call with budget owner *before* AE handoff. Do not ask influencer to make the intro and hope it sticks.
- **Decision Process Vague**: Prospect says "we'll just run it by leadership." Action: "Who specifically is on your leadership team? Who decides on software purchases?"
- **Paper Process Unknown**: Prospect says "we'll handle it." Action: "Will there be a procurement team, security review, or legal review? Who owns those?"
- **Champion Weak**: Prospect is neutral or you haven't identified a champion. Action: "Who on your team will feel the most impact if we solve this?" (find the person with skin in the game)

---

### Scoring Rules

- **Don't inflate scores for rapport**: If a prospect is friendly but gave vague answers, score = 1 (Unknown), not 2 (Partial)
- **Don't score from inference**: If prospect didn't explicitly say budget exists, don't assume Economic Buyer is Strong
- **Partial ≠ Good**: Partial means you have some data but gaps remain. If two elements are Partial, that's material risk in deal progression
- **Unknown ≠ Partial**: Unknown = you asked and got an evasive answer. Partial = you got some data but not all. These are different signals
- **If SAP < 14, do not escalate to AE**: Schedule a second discovery call to close Critical gaps (E, I, C Champion)

---

### Decision Tree: To Handoff or Not?

```
Score all 8 elements (0–3 each)

SAP ≥ 20?
├─ YES → Hand off immediately. AE confirms in discovery.
├─ NO, SAP 14–19? → Hand off. Prep AE with gaps. Flag weakest elements.
└─ NO, SAP 12–13?
   ├─ Is E (Econ Buyer) or I (Identify Pain) at 0 or 1? → Do NOT hand off. Schedule follow-up.
   ├─ Is Decision Process or Paper Process at 0 or 1? → Hand off only if other 5 are 2+. Flag risk.
   └─ Otherwise → Manager review. If you've built rapport, might be worth one more call.

SAP < 12?
└─ Do NOT hand off. Schedule follow-up discovery. Prospect not ready.
```

### Translation of MEDDPICC Terms for Larger Buying Committees

When prospect says "we don't have one buyer," map to their structure:
- **Economic Buyer** = "Budget owner" (CFO, VP Finance, Head of Procurement)
- **Champion** = "Internal sponsor" or "power user" (person who'll evangelize)
- **Decision Criteria** = "Evaluation framework" or "scoring rubric"
- **Paper Process** = "Sign-off checklist" or "approval gates"

## Example

**Scenario: SaaS sales intelligence tool. SDR (you) on call with Maya, a Marketing Director at a Series-B fintech.**

**Call transcript (excerpts):**

*You: "Maya, if we solve the challenge of getting accurate lead scoring, what does that mean for your team?"*

*Maya: "Honestly, we're losing maybe 20–30% of qualified leads to bad data. If we fixed that, we'd probably add $500k in annual revenue just from improving our close rate."*

→ **Metrics: 3 (Strong)** — Specific impact quantified ($500k revenue)

---

*You: "Who would need to approve a tool like this?"*

*Maya: "That would be our CFO, James. He owns software spend. I've mentioned the problem to him but haven't formally asked about budget."*

→ **Economic Buyer: 2 (Partial)** — Identified but not directly confirmed; budget status unclear

---

*You: "When you evaluate a solution, what matters most?"*

*Maya: "We need it to integrate with Salesforce—that's non-negotiable. Second, price matters; we're bootstrapped so under $50k/year. Third, onboarding speed; we need to move fast. And honestly, the vendor's reputation in fintech helps."*

→ **Decision Criteria: 3 (Strong)** — Four clear criteria with priority ranking

---

*You: "Walk me through how this would get approved. Are there other steps beyond James?"*

*Maya: "Yeah, so James would approve spend. Our VP of Sales would need to sign off on the solution itself. And because we handle financial data, there's a security review—that usually takes 2 weeks. Then legal would do a quick contract review, maybe 1 week. Timeline? Probably 4–5 weeks from decision to go."*

→ **Decision Process: 3 (Strong)** — Stakeholders, timeline, and structure clear
→ **Paper Process: 3 (Strong)** — Security review (2 weeks), legal (1 week), procurement owners identified

---

*You: "What's driving the urgency around this? What happens if you don't solve the lead-scoring problem?"*

*Maya: "We're ramping sales in Q3. If we don't fix lead quality now, we'll miss our pipeline targets by probably 25–30%. James knows this is a risk."*

→ **Identify Pain: 3 (Strong)** — Specific quantified pain (25–30% pipeline miss) and consequence (Q3 miss)

---

*You: "Who on your team would be most excited about this solution?"*

*Maya: "Definitely our Head of Sales, David. He's been pushing for better data for a year. If I show him a viable solution, he'll fight for it internally."*

→ **Champion: 3 (Strong)** — Identified, motivated, and positioned to evangelize

---

*You: "Are you evaluating other vendors for this?"*

*Maya: "We looked at Clearbit last year, but their pricing jumped. We're also in early talks with [competitor X], who's cheaper but doesn't integrate as cleanly with Salesforce. Your integration story is better."*

→ **Competition: 3 (Strong)** — Named competitors, stated pros/cons

---

**SAP SCORECARD:**
```
M — Metrics:        3 ($500k revenue impact)
E — Econ Buyer:     2 (Identified but not confirmed)
D — Decision Crit:  3 (4 criteria, prioritized)
D — Decision Proc:  3 (Stakeholders + timeline)
P — Paper Process:  3 (Security + legal + timeline)
I — Identify Pain:  3 (25–30% pipeline miss, Q3 consequence)
C — Champion:       3 (David, Head of Sales, motivated)
C — Competition:    3 (Clearbit, Competitor X, comparison stated)

TOTAL SAP SCORE: 23/24
```

**Decision**: Hand off immediately to AE. This deal is enterprise-ready.

**HANDOFF BRIEF to AE:**
```
Prospect: Maya Lopez, Marketing Director, Series-B Fintech
Economic Buyer: James (CFO) — NOT YET SPOKEN TO
Champion: David (Head of Sales) — Motivated, will evangelize
SAP Score: 23/24

Key Metrics: $500k revenue impact from fixing lead quality; 25–30% pipeline miss if not solved by Q3

Gaps to confirm in discovery:
- James' appetite for spend (Maya hasn't formally asked yet)
- David's timeline for solution (must align with Q3 ramp)

Next: Schedule demo with Maya + David; loop James in for budget discussion.

Risk flags: None. Strong deal.
```

---

This prospect clears the handoff threshold with a 23/24 SAP. The AE's job is to lock in Economic Buyer approval and confirm timeline.
