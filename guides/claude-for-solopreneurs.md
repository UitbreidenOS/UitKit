# Claude for Solopreneurs

Claude for solopreneurs is built for the operator who is the sales team, the support team, the bookkeeper, and the founder — all in one person, all in one week. This guide covers how to use Claude (the AI from Anthropic) to compress 8-15 hours of weekly mechanical work into 1-2 hours of review work, without hiring, without a developer, and without changing the tools you already pay for.

If you run a one-person business, a side-business plus a day job, or a two-person LLC, this guide is for you. The skills below are written for plain-English use — no terminal, no API keys, no setup more complicated than connecting QuickBooks once.

---

## What Claude does for a solopreneur

A solopreneur typically spends their week on six recurring activities:

1. Sales and outreach — finding leads, writing first-touch emails, following up
2. Delivery — doing the actual work the client paid for
3. Admin — invoicing, expenses, reconciliation, taxes
4. Marketing — newsletter, social, content
5. Customer service — answering questions, handling complaints, scheduling
6. Strategy — pricing, positioning, deciding what to focus on next

Of those six, only delivery cannot be assisted by Claude in a meaningful way (and even there, Claude helps with drafts, research, and review). Every other activity has at least one Claudient skill purpose-built for the solopreneur use case.

## How a solopreneur sets it up

Setup takes 90 minutes spread across two evenings. Don't try to do it all in one session.

### Evening 1 — Subscribe and connect (45 minutes)

1. **Claude Pro at $20/month** is enough for most solopreneurs. You'll move to Claude Max ($100/month) only if you run 6+ workflows daily on large datasets.
2. **Open Claude Cowork** from your Claude dashboard. This is the GUI layer where the small-business workflows live — no terminal, no CLI.
3. **Connect QuickBooks Online first.** It unlocks Invoice Chaser, Month-End Close, Cash Flow Forecast, Payroll Planner, Expense Audit, and Tax Organizer in one OAuth click.
4. **Write your Business Context document.** This is the highest-leverage 30 minutes you'll spend. 300-400 words describing what you do, who you do it for, your tone of voice, and the words you use in your industry. Every workflow reads this.

### Evening 2 — First workflow and rhythm (45 minutes)

1. **Run Invoice Chaser first.** It's the workflow with the clearest ROI — you can see exactly how much money is in your AR aging report. Run it, read every draft email, send the ones that look right.
2. **Schedule Monday Brief.** Pin it to your Monday morning routine for 4 weeks. The discipline of starting the week with one structured 5-minute read sets the tone for every workflow that follows.
3. **Connect HubSpot if you sell.** Free tier is fine. It unlocks Lead Triager, Cold Outreach, Email Campaign.
4. **Stop there.** Don't activate a third workflow this week. Habits stack one at a time.

## What to expect: solopreneur 30/60/90

### Days 1-30: The first workflow becomes habit

You run Invoice Chaser weekly. You run Monday Brief every Monday. You catch yourself reviewing Claude drafts faster as you trust them more. You've saved 4-6 hours per week, mostly on invoice follow-up and weekly status review.

The first time Claude mis-scores a lead or writes an invoice email too aggressively, you'll notice. That's the review step doing its job. The next run, the context is sharper.

### Days 31-60: The stack expands

You add Cash Flow Forecast and Lead Triager. Your Monday now starts with 15 minutes of: pulse, cash, leads. You make better decisions because you start the week with three structured reads instead of opening four tabs and hoping.

Time saved: 8-10 hours per week. You're starting to feel the back of your week.

### Days 61-90: Compound

You add Content Repurposer or Campaign Brief if marketing is in your week. You add SOP Writer if you're starting to think about contracting out delivery work. You're now running 5-6 workflows on a weekly cadence.

You spend Friday afternoon doing the work you actually wanted the business for — designing the next thing — instead of catching up on admin. Time saved per week: 10-14 hours.

## Solopreneur-specific success patterns

**Build a Monday ritual.** The single highest-impact change is starting the week with one structured 5-15 minute review. Monday Brief, Cash Flow Forecast, Lead Triager. In that order. By 10am Monday you know the three things to focus on.

**Use Claude for the worst hours of your week.** Identify your two worst hours — the ones you procrastinate on, the ones that pile up. For most solopreneurs that's invoice chasing and inbox triage. Those are the workflows to activate first.

**Treat the business context document as living.** Re-read it every 60 days. Your pricing changes, your ICP refines, your tone evolves. The document should evolve too. Claude is calibrated to whatever the document says today.

**Don't try to replace yourself on judgment.** Lead scores are inputs, not decisions. Cash forecasts assume your customer behavior keeps doing what it's been doing — Claude doesn't know your largest customer is about to leave. Use Claude for the mechanical layer; keep the judgment for yourself.

## What Claude is NOT for as a solopreneur

**Legal and tax decisions.** Tax Organizer prepares the CPA packet. It does not advise on entity structure, deduction strategy, or whether to expense versus depreciate a purchase. That's your CPA.

**Pricing decisions you haven't thought through.** Pricing Optimizer (see `skills/small-business/pricing-optimizer.md`) is a structured way to test what you already suspect. It is not a way to outsource the strategic decision of who you want to be.

**Replacing customer relationships.** Customer Inquiry handles FAQs and after-hours triage. It does not handle the call where your biggest client says they're unhappy. That's yours.

**Anything you'd be embarrassed to send.** If you wouldn't sign off on a Claude draft without a review, the review is the point. Skipping it doesn't save time over the long run.

## FAQ

### Is Claude good for solopreneurs?

Yes. Claude is purpose-built for the solopreneur use case — owner-initiated, approval-based, no developer required. The 15 official Claude for Small Business workflows plus the long tail of Claudient skills cover roughly 60-80% of the recurring weekly work a solo operator does.

### How is Claude different from ChatGPT for solopreneurs?

ChatGPT is a generalist chat assistant. Claude (specifically Claude for Small Business inside Claude Cowork) connects to your actual business tools — QuickBooks, HubSpot, PayPal, Google Workspace — and produces outputs grounded in your real data. ChatGPT can write a generic invoice reminder. Claude reads your actual aging report and drafts personalized reminders by invoice. The difference compounds across every workflow.

### How much does Claude cost for a solopreneur?

$20/month for Claude Pro covers the use case for most one-person businesses. $100/month for Claude Max if you run more than 6 workflows daily or work with large financial datasets. Everything else — QuickBooks, HubSpot Free, PayPal — you already pay for.

### Do I need to know how to code?

No. Claude for Small Business is point-and-click inside Claude Cowork. The skills in this Claudient repo are activated by typing plain English instructions to Claude. The only setup is OAuth-connecting your existing tools, which takes a few clicks per tool.

### Can Claude read my QuickBooks data?

Yes, once you authorize the QuickBooks Online integration via OAuth. Claude reads your invoices, transactions, customers, and reports at the moment a workflow runs. It does not poll your account in the background and is not retained for model training.

### What if I use Stripe or Xero instead of QuickBooks?

The native Claude for Small Business workflows are most complete with QuickBooks Online. Xero and Stripe integrations are improving but not yet at parity. If you use those tools, you can still use Claude Cowork's general document and email assistance, but the financial workflows will have reduced functionality.

### Can Claude actually replace a bookkeeper?

No, and you shouldn't want it to. Claude prepares the reconciliation, organizes the receipts, and drafts the P&L. Your bookkeeper or CPA reviews and signs off. The combined cost is lower than a bookkeeper alone, and the turnaround is faster.

### Is Claude for Small Business worth it for a solo founder?

If you spend 6+ hours per week on the activities Claude covers — invoicing, lead follow-up, weekly reporting, content repurposing, customer FAQs — yes. Most solopreneurs reach a 3-5x return within 60 days. If you spend less time on those activities (because you've already automated them another way, or because they aren't part of your business), the math may not work.
