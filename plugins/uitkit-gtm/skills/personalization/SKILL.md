# Personalization

## When to activate
Before writing any cold outreach message; when building account research playbooks; when scoring personalization quality on a message or campaign; when deciding resource allocation for a prospect pool.

## When NOT to use
Templated mass email campaigns where personalization isn't operationally feasible; campaigns targeting thousands of accounts simultaneously where per-account research is not economical; internal communication that doesn't require external research; warm outreach where you already have credible context.

## Instructions

### The 6-Bucket Personalization Framework

The framework ranks personalization sources by research depth and signal strength. Each bucket represents a different quality tier and time investment. Use the highest bucket available given your time and account tier.

#### Bucket 1: Self-Authored Content (Highest Signal)
Their podcast episodes, published articles, LinkedIn posts they wrote, books, conference talks, demo videos, or AMAs. This is the strongest personalization signal because it reveals their current thinking, priorities, and voice.

**Research approach:** Search their name + "podcast guest," check their personal blog, LinkedIn search for longer-form posts, Google Scholar for papers, YouTube for talks.

**Personalization strength:** 9/10 — Direct evidence of their priorities and perspective.

**Time investment:** 15–30 minutes for one detailed reference.

---

#### Bucket 2: Company News (Strong Signal)
Recent funding announcements, product launches, public roadmap updates, hiring surges, executive promotions, acquisition activity, or public leadership changes. These are timely and specific, indicating direction and priorities.

**Research approach:** Company press releases, TechCrunch/CrunchBase for funding, LinkedIn company page updates, recent job postings surge, company blog announcements, industry news wires.

**Personalization strength:** 8/10 — Objective, verifiable, recent context.

**Time investment:** 10–20 minutes per prospect.

---

#### Bucket 3: Role-Specific Triggers (Moderate-Strong Signal)
Job posting keywords matching your ICP, recent LinkedIn activity spikes, promotion to a new role in the last 3 months, LinkedIn skill endorsements in relevant areas, or speaker activity in adjacent spaces. These signal active role/responsibility changes.

**Research approach:** LinkedIn role history and dates, recent job postings on their careers page, LinkedIn activity feed, speaker bios on conference sites.

**Personalization strength:** 7/10 — Recent but inferred context.

**Time investment:** 5–15 minutes per prospect.

---

#### Bucket 4: Industry Signals (Moderate Signal)
Regulatory changes affecting their vertical, competitor moves, market events (economic shifts, acquisitions, consolidation), industry event attendance, or analyst reports about their sector. These provide conversation hooks at the macro level.

**Research approach:** Industry news, regulatory filings (SEC, EU), analyst reports (Gartner, Forrester, IDC), industry event schedules, competitor press releases.

**Personalization strength:** 6/10 — Contextual but non-specific to the individual.

**Time investment:** 5–10 minutes, often reusable across an entire vertical or segment.

---

#### Bucket 5: Mutual Connection or Shared Community (Moderate Signal)
A warm introduction angle, shared industry association membership, alumni network, conference attendance, or community participation (Slack groups, forums, meetups). These create credibility through network proximity.

**Research approach:** LinkedIn connection search, company website team pages for alumni, conference attendee lists, community member directories, affinity group memberships.

**Personalization strength:** 6/10 — Relationship-based credibility signal.

**Time investment:** 3–10 minutes if warm intro available; 10–20 if discovering shared community.

---

#### Bucket 6: Core-Static Relevance (Baseline Signal)
ICP fit alone: company size, industry vertical, geography, technology stack, use case alignment. No fresh research. This is the fallback—always available but weakest signal.

**Research approach:** Company website, Crunchbase, LinkedIn company page (static data).

**Personalization strength:** 3/10 — Generic but defensible.

**Time investment:** 1–3 minutes (often pre-recorded).

---

### How to Choose Which Bucket to Use: Time + Tier Logic

**Account Tier & Time Available Decision Tree:**

| Account Tier | Time Per Prospect | Primary Buckets | Fallback |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Tier 1 (High-Value, Low Volume)** | 30+ min | Buckets 1, 2, 3 | Bucket 4 |
| **Tier 2 (Mid-Market, Moderate Volume)** | 15–20 min | Buckets 2, 3, 5 | Bucket 4 |
| **Tier 3 (SMB, High Volume)** | 5–10 min | Buckets 3, 5, 6 | Bucket 6 |
| **Tier 4 (Enterprise, Highest Value)** | 45+ min | Buckets 1, 2, 3, 4 (layered) | Any previous |

**Operational Rule:**
- If you find Bucket 1 material, use it—it overrides all others.
- If not, move to the next available bucket in order (2→3→4→5→6).
- Never skip a bucket you find unless time pressure explicitly requires it.
- For warm intros (Bucket 5), always prioritize; it changes the opening dynamic entirely.

---

### The Personalization Paragraph Formula

Every cold outreach message should contain a personalization paragraph that follows this three-step structure:

**[Hook from Bucket] + [Bridge to Your Value Prop] + [Specific Ask]**

**Breakdown:**

1. **Hook (2–3 sentences):** The specific insight or observation from whichever bucket you're using. Make it specific—not "I saw you're in sales" but "I noticed you launched the product bundles feature last quarter on your roadmap."

2. **Bridge (1–2 sentences):** A conditional statement connecting their context to your solution. Format: "Given [their situation], [relevant insight about how you help similar people]."

3. **Specific Ask (1–2 sentences):** A precise, low-friction request. Not "let's grab coffee" but "would a 20-minute call to discuss [specific topic] make sense this week?"

**Formula Template:**

> [Hook: "I noticed {specific observation from research}"]
>
> [Bridge: "Given this context, {companies/people we work with} typically {outcome or challenge you solve}."]
>
> [Specific Ask: "Worth a quick conversation about {concrete topic}? I'm thinking {specific angle}."]

---

### Research Depth Checklist

Before sending any cold outreach, validate that your personalization sits in at least Bucket 3 or higher:

- [ ] Is there a specific, verifiable fact in the first sentence (not a generic observation)?
- [ ] Does my hook demonstrate research beyond the company's homepage?
- [ ] Can the prospect immediately recognize themselves in my message (specific name, role, recent event)?
- [ ] Does my bridge create a logical connection to my ask, not a leap?
- [ ] Is my ask so specific that it shows I've thought about *their* constraints, not just mine?

If you check fewer than 4 boxes, drop to the next bucket and redo your research.

---

### Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Bucket Mixing (Weak):** Combining multiple weak signals to fake depth. "I see you're in SaaS, in Boston, and hiring" (all Bucket 6). Better to own one Bucket 3 signal than pretend three Bucket 6 signals add up.

2. **Generic Hook, Specific Wrapper:** Writing "I see you're in sales ops" then pivoting to a generic value prop. The hook must be *specifically* theirs.

3. **Skipping the Bridge:** Jumping directly from observation to ask without explaining *why* your solution matters to their specific situation.

4. **Asking Too Much:** Asking for 30 minutes when a Bucket 6 signal warrants a 15-minute ask.

5. **Over-Researching Lower Buckets:** Spending 45 minutes on Bucket 5 (mutual connection) research when you could have found Bucket 2 (company news) in 10 minutes.

## Example

### Scenario
You are an Account Executive at a compliance automation platform (SaaS). Your target is **Sarah Chen**, VP of Operations at HealthTech Logistics, a mid-market logistics company. You have 20 minutes to research and 15 minutes to write.

### Your Research Process

**Bucket 1 check (5 min):** Search "Sarah Chen" + "HealthTech Logistics" + "podcast" or "article" → No results. Skip.

**Bucket 2 check (10 min):** 
- HealthTech Logistics announced a Series B funding round 2 months ago ($35M).
- Recently launched a white-glove service tier for pharma logistics (requires FDA compliance documentation).
- Hired 3 compliance specialists in the last 90 days (visible on LinkedIn jobs page).

**Bucket 3 check (3 min):** 
- Sarah's role changed from "Operations Manager" to "VP of Operations" 8 weeks ago.
- No recent personal LinkedIn posts, but her profile updated to reflect expanded scope.

**Decision:** Use Bucket 2 (company news: Series B + pharma expansion + new compliance hires) + Bucket 3 (recent promotion).

### Your Outreach Message

**Subject:** Scaling compliance for pharma logistics (Series B growth)

---

Hi Sarah,

I came across your Series B announcement and noticed you've just launched the pharma white-glove service—that's a big play, and it immediately caught my attention because it means you've probably hit a wall: FDA documentation requirements don't scale with generic logistics ops.

I work with mid-market logistics companies scaling into regulated verticals. The blocker is always the same—your compliance team gets bottlenecked on document collection and audit trails, especially when you're ramping up new service tiers. We've cut that process from weeks to days for companies like you.

Given your recent promotion to VP of Operations and the new compliance hiring I saw on your jobs page, this might be a good time to talk. Would a 20-minute conversation about how other companies in your position are handling pharma compliance automation make sense this week?

Sarah

---

**Breakdown:**

| Element | Source | Quality |
|---|---|---|
| **Hook** | Bucket 2 + Bucket 3: Series B announcement + new VP role + pharma expansion + compliance hires | Specific and recent |
| **Bridge** | "FDA documentation + scaling + bottleneck insight" shows domain knowledge | Credible positioning |
| **Ask** | "20 minutes this week" on a concrete topic (pharma compliance automation) | Low-friction, specific |
| **Overall** | Sits in Bucket 2–3 range; shows 20 minutes of research; targeted, not generic | High quality for time spent |
